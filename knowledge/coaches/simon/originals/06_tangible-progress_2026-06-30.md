# Tangible Progress

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

Here's a nice collocation: tangible progress (e.g. I'm making tangible progress)
Tangible means real and definite, or something that you can touch.
This is the type of progress that we should be aiming for. But how can you make your IELTS preparation progress more tangible? What can you do each day that gives you a sense of real improvement?
If you want to make tangible daily progress, try to do one of the following:
Analyse one grammar or vocabulary mistake that you made, and understand the correction.
Analyse one mistake in a reading or listening test, and understand why you got the answer wrong.
Learn one new word, collocation or phrase.
Learn how to express one idea in a 'natural' way. Find out how a native speaker would say it.
Prepare ideas, opinions and vocabulary for one IELTS topic.
Read one sample band 9 essay, and highlight anything useful e.g. vocabulary, cohesive devices, how ideas are developed.
Write one paragraph and get it checked by a teacher.
Record yourself doing one speaking test. Transcribe the recording and analyse the transcription or get it checked by a teacher.
I could add more points, but I'm sure you get the idea. Just do one thing that feels like real progress every day. Imagine where you'll be after 100 days!
