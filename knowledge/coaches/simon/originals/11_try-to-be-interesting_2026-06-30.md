# IELTS Advice: try to be interesting!

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

In this week's speaking lesson I answered the "Describe a vehicle you would like to own" task by describing a helicopter.
I don't really want to own a helicopter, but I thought it would be much more interesting to choose this instead of a car. An extreme or unrealistic choice, like my helicopter, forces you to use your imagination, which usually leads to a more memorable description.
So, how can you learn to be more interesting when you speak or write? Here are a few suggestions:
1. Be extreme or unrealistic (see above)
2. Give 'named' examples (e.g. tigers and rhinos in my first answer here)
3. Give personal examples (e.g. paragraph 2 here)
4. Tell an anecdote
5. Describe in detail (e.g. paragraphs 2 and 3 here)
In other words, write or say something that isn't general, vague or bland.
Please note: Being interesting isn't a 'rule', and it isn't mentioned in the band descriptors for writing or speaking. This is just my advice!
