# Idea: Commercialisation erodes cultural authenticity

## Theme
culture-tourism

## Learning Status
New

## Core Idea
When a culture or tradition faces heavy commercial demand from outsiders, it tends to
get simplified, mass-produced, and packaged as a "product" — losing its original
authenticity in the process.

## Skeleton

| Step | Content |
|---|---|
| Claim | Increased demand from outsiders leads to a traditional practice being commercialised |
| Reason | Commercial production is optimised for volume and broad appeal, not for preserving nuance or meaning |
| Mechanism | The more a tradition is scaled up to meet outside demand, the more its original context and depth get stripped away to make it easier to sell — authenticity is sacrificed for mass appeal/profit |
| Impact | Cultural erosion / loss of authenticity |
| Example | Traditional crafts that once took skilled artisans days to make are replaced by cheap, mass-produced souvenir versions sold to tourists, who often cannot tell the difference |

## Reusable IELTS Angle
Standard negative angle for any "tradition vs commercialisation/globalisation"
conflict question — the disadvantage counterpart to demand-driven growth arguments.

## Speaking Use
Part 3 answers to "Do you think tourism/globalisation has a negative effect on local
culture?": "In some cases, yes — when there's a lot of demand from outsiders, local
traditions can end up being simplified or mass-produced just to sell to tourists,
which means they lose some of their original meaning."

## Useful Chunks
- "sacrificed for mass appeal"
- "mass-produced versions"
- "loses its original meaning/authenticity"
