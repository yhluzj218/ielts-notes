# IELTS Advice: Changing your approach

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

Here's a recent comment that I received from a member of this site:
Hi Simon. I am a student from China. Until I saw your lessons on the website, I didn't understand that in my over ten years of English study, I have been falling into misunderstandings by many Chinese teachers, especially in the aspect of writing study. Chinese teachers pay too much attention to complex sentence patterns and grammar. Your articles have taught me that even with basic grammar and vocabulary, it is possible to write a band 9 essay.
I want to say two things about this comment.
First, I'm very happy that my lessons have changed this student's approach to IELTS preparation and learning English.
Second, I wouldn't say that my approach is to use "basic" grammar and vocabulary. I'm trying to teach people to speak and write in a more natural, native-speaker style. So my focus is on clarity, coherence, development of ideas, topic vocabulary, collocations and having a method for each part of the test. My essays may look easy or basic, but they are not; it takes practice and careful thinking to write in the style that I use.
However, I do agree that you are more likely to get a high score if you follow my approach, as opposed to the "memorising complex structures and big words" approach!
Note: I wouldn't use the phrase below. Can you suggest how we could rewrite it?
"I have been falling into misunderstandings by many Chinese teachers"
My suggestions:
I have been misled by the advice of certain Chinese teachers.
Many Chinese teachers have given me misleading advice.
I've been preparing for the IELTS test in the wrong way because of the misinformation that many Chinese teachers propagate*.
*Notice the nice collocation: to propagate misinformation
