# Idea: No long-term stake leads to irresponsible behaviour

## Theme
government-society

## Core Idea
When a person only participates in a place or system briefly, and won't experience the
long-term consequences of their actions, they tend to behave less responsibly.

## Why
Responsibility is usually reinforced by having to live with the outcome. Remove that
future consequence — because you're leaving soon, or won't be affected personally —
and the incentive to act carefully weakens.

## Example
Tourists litter or behave carelessly in a destination because they won't be the ones
living with the consequences, unlike local residents.

## Reusable IELTS Angle
General causal explanation for "why does group X behave irresponsibly/badly" —
not limited to tourism, works for any short-term-participation scenario.

## Writing Use
Body paragraph chain:
`[Point] because [group] only stay/participate for a short time → [Explain] they don't experience the long-term consequences of their actions → [Example] tourists litter or behave badly since they won't live with the result → [Result] the cost is borne by others (residents/environment/future)`

## Speaking Use
Part 3 answers to "Why do some people not care about the environment / their
surroundings?": "I think part of it is that if you're only somewhere temporarily, you
don't really feel the long-term impact of your actions, so there's less incentive to
be careful."

## Useful Chunks
- "don't experience the long-term consequences"
- "won't live with the result"
- "the cost is borne by others"
