# Advice: Think in paragraphs

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

I was reading a student's answer to a speaking part 2 task earlier today, and the first thing that I noticed was that there were no paragraphs.
You might ask: Do paragraphs matter when we're speaking?
My answer is that paragraphs always matter. Although nobody sees your paragraphing when you are speaking, I recommend that you always think in terms of paragraphs.
This means that you start with an idea, and then you explain it in detail. When you come to the end of one point or idea, there will usually be a short pause in your speech before you begin the next point. It's just like starting a new paragraph.
Whether you're writing, speaking or even just thinking about something, it always helps to think in paragraphs.
And remember: clear, coherent thinking is the secret to clear, coherent writing and speaking.
