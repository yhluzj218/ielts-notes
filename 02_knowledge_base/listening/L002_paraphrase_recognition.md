# L002 — Paraphrase Recognition

## Core Rule

IELTS Listening questions use **different words** from the recording. The recording uses a paraphrase of the answer. Your job is to recognise the same meaning in different words.

> Question: `fast movement`
> Recording: `rapid motion`

These mean the same thing. If you only listen for `fast` and `movement`, you miss it.

## Why it matters in IELTS

Paraphrasing is the primary way IELTS tests listening comprehension. It is present in every section but especially frequent in S3 and S4.

## Common paraphrase types

| Type | Question word | Recording word |
|---|---|---|
| Synonym | `expensive` | `costly` / `pricey` |
| Verb ↔ noun | `to reduce` | `a reduction in` |
| Active ↔ passive | `they built` | `was constructed` |
| Specific ↔ general | `scientists` | `researchers` |
| Formal ↔ informal | `assist` | `help` |

## Strategy

1. During pre-read: for each question, think of 2–3 synonyms for the key words
2. While listening: don't wait for the exact words from the question — listen for the meaning
3. Note: topic-related vocabulary often stays the same; functional vocabulary changes

## Mini practice

Match the question phrase to its likely recording paraphrase:

| Question | Recording paraphrase |
|---|---|
| `ancient ruins` | a) `an old structure` / `historical remains` |
| `children enjoy` | b) `young people find it fun` / `kids are keen on` |
| `increase in cost` | c) `prices have risen` / `more expensive` |
