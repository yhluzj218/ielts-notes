# Model Essay：Print vs Digital News

**日期：** 2026-06-19
**類型：** Task 2

## 題目

In the future, nobody will buy printed newspapers or books because they will be able to read everything they want online without paying.

To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?

## 範本

Some people have suggested that in the near or distant future people will stop purchasing physical newspapers or books since everything will be available to read online for free. However, I firmly oppose this idea for two important reasons.

First and foremost, it seems unlikely that everyone will stop reading newspapers and books in their physical form. This is because many people enjoy the benefits of reading a real newspaper or book such as the ability to highlight important information or the feeling of turning a page. For instance, many students find that it is imperative to purchase a paper copy of their learning materials so that they can annotate and colour code crucial details. Even though certain people will switch to digital-only books or newspapers in the coming decades, there will still be a significant part of society who will use hard copies.

Another important issue with the proposed idea is that not all content will be freely available online. It is clear to see that many newspaper articles have already been placed behind a paywall and the vast majority of ebooks are sold for similar prices to their paper equivalents. The New York Times, The Economist and Kindle books are three well-known examples of paid digital content and it seems unlikely that they will ever change such a successful business model. It is true that certain organisations and authors may choose to make their content free by focusing on advertising revenue, despite this it seems clear that people will need to pay to access valuable information in the future.

To sum up, despite the increasing trend towards digital media, I strongly disagree with both the idea that physical newspapers and books will no longer be bought by individuals and the reason given that all information online will be available for free.

## 這篇好在哪（我要學的結構/手法）

1. **開頭就表態，且預告結構**
   > "I firmly oppose this idea for two important reasons."
   立場 + reasons 數量都在第一段末句交代，考官立刻知道你在幹嘛。

2. **題目改寫沒有偷換強度**
   "nobody will buy" → "people will stop purchasing"，保留了「全面停止」的意思，沒有縮水成 "fewer people"。

3. **兩個 body 段各攻題目的一個主張**
   - Body 1 反駁「沒人會買實體」
   - Body 2 反駁「一切都免費」
   這叫「回應題目的每一個論點」，Task Response 直接拿高分。

4. **每個 body 段都有讓步句，再轉回去**
   不是只說自己的理由，而是先承認對方有一點道理，再說「但仍然…」——這讓論證更有說服力，結構也更成熟。

5. **例子具體，用品牌名取代模糊描述**
   "The New York Times, The Economist and Kindle books" 比 "some companies" 有力，而且不需要任何罕見詞彙。

## 值得學的表達（自然、可借用）

| 表達 | 中文說明 | 適用情境 |
|------|----------|----------|
| `it seems unlikely that...` | 「似乎不太可能…」，語氣有保留但不軟弱 | 反駁一個預測時，開啟 body 段 |
| `placed behind a paywall` | 「設置付費牆」，是描述線上內容收費的標準說法 | 討論媒體、內容產業相關題目 |
| `sold for similar prices to their paper equivalents` | 「售價和實體版差不多」，equivalents 自然替代 "same thing" | 比較實體與數位版本 |
| `a significant part of society` | 「社會上相當大一部分人」，比 "many people" 有份量 | 說明某族群仍有需求時 |
| `it is imperative to` | 「有必要…／不得不…」，強調必要性 | 舉例說明某行為為何重要 |
| `focusing on advertising revenue` | 「靠廣告收入維持」，一個字組說清楚商業模式 | 討論媒體經營、免費內容相關題目 |

⚠️ 以上都是「在對的情境自然用出來」的表達，不是要你硬塞。

## 連接詞 / 句型片語（可直接套用）

| 片語 | 功能 / 文章位置 |
|------|----------------|
| `However, I firmly oppose this idea for two important reasons.` | **Intro 末句表態**：改寫完題目後，用 However 轉入自己立場 |
| `First and foremost,` | **Body 1 開頭**：比 Firstly 更有力，用於第一個、最重要的論點 |
| `This is because...` | **展開理由**：緊接在論點句後，直接解釋原因 |
| `For instance,` | **舉例**：引入具體例子，比 For example 更簡潔 |
| `Even though..., there will still be...` | **Body 內讓步**：承認部分人會改變，但強調仍有人不會 |
| `Another important issue with the proposed idea is that...` | **Body 2 開頭**：轉入第二個論點，同時呼應「題目提出的主張」 |
| `It is clear to see that...` | **補充論據**：引入已知事實或趨勢，語氣肯定 |
| `It is true that..., despite this it seems clear that...` | **Body 內讓步 + 轉折**：承認例外後拉回主論點，是成熟論證的核心句型 |
| `To sum up, despite..., I strongly disagree with both... and...` | **結尾**：重申立場，同時點名反駁了題目的兩個主張 |
