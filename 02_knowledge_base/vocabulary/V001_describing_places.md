# V001 — Vocabulary: Describing Places (Part 2)

## Core Rule

These three words have **specific meanings** that are often confused. Use them only in the correct context.

## The Three Words

### `pristine`

**Meaning:** Completely untouched by humans — no buildings, no pollution, no litter.

❌ Wrong: "The beach was pristine — they just built a new hotel there." (building present → not pristine)
✅ Right: "The coastline was pristine — no roads, no buildings, just untouched nature."

**Use when:** describing wilderness, untouched beaches, virgin forest

---

### `artificial`

**Meaning:** Man-made, does not fit naturally into the environment. Often implies it stands out or looks out of place.

✅ "The artificial lights made the park feel unnatural at night."
✅ "The resort's artificial beach looked nothing like the real thing."

**Use when:** describing something made by humans that contrasts with the natural environment

---

### `well-preserved`

**Meaning:** Looked after carefully, so the original appearance/quality/structure is still visible.

✅ "The old town was well-preserved — you could still see the original colonial architecture."
✅ "The ancient ruins were surprisingly well-preserved."

**Use when:** describing historical sites, old buildings, natural areas that have been maintained

---

## Choosing between them

| Situation | Word |
|---|---|
| No human contact at all | `pristine` |
| Humans have been there but look after it | `well-preserved` |
| Humans made something that looks out of place | `artificial` |

## Reusable patterns

```
"The area was completely pristine — [no sign of human activity]."
"Despite being [number] years old, the [place] was remarkably well-preserved."
"The [structure] looked artificial against the natural landscape."
```
