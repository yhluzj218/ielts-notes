## Suno Prompt

Use my saved voice profile.

Create a simple and cheerful English nursery-rhyme learning chant for IELTS
Writing sentence-pattern practice, shadowing, pronunciation training, and
long-term memory.

Use a slow-to-medium tempo. Do not make it fast. Give every short phrase enough
space to repeat comfortably, with clear pauses between lines for shadowing.

Use light acoustic guitar, gentle hand claps, soft shaker, and simple percussion.
Keep the melody very simple and the rhythm strong and steady.

Use a clear, natural female lead vocal. The delivery should be mostly
spoken-singing with a light melody and a simple call-and-response feeling. Every
word must be easy to hear. Carefully articulate consonants and word endings,
especially "related," "fact," and the final words of each clause. Avoid slurred
words, excessive connected speech, mumbling, and breathy singing.

Repeat the pattern "Related to this is the fact that [clause]" many times through
short, natural IELTS examples. Emphasise the rhythm of "Related to this," "is the
fact that," and the linked clause after it. Make each line easy to repeat after
one hearing.

Add light, simple background harmonies only in the chorus. Keep them soft and
unobtrusive. The lead vocal must always remain louder and clearer than the
backing vocals.

The mood should be warm, friendly, playful, relaxed, and memorable. Prioritise
clarity over speed, repetition over musical variety, and intelligibility over
performance.

No sleepy ballad. No dramatic singing. No strong vibrato. No belting. No jazz
improvisation. No complex vocal runs. No electronic dance beat. No cartoon voice.
Do not imitate any real singer or artist.
