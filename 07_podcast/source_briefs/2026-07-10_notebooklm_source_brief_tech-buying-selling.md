# Source Briefing Article — New Technology and the Way We Buy and Sell

## IELTS Question

New technologies and ways of buying and selling are transforming the lives of consumers. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this opinion? Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.

## Question Type

Opinion (Agree / Disagree)

This is an Opinion question because it asks directly "to what extent do you agree or disagree," requiring a clear personal position defended with reasons and examples, rather than a discussion of both sides for its own sake.

## Background

A hundred years ago, buying something meant going to a local shop, and finding something specific might mean visiting several shops or travelling further. Today, purchasing has changed dramatically: consumers can buy almost anything with a few taps on a smartphone. According to UN Trade and Development (UNCTAD), e-commerce is now one of the fastest-growing parts of the global economy, expanding faster than overall GDP, with growth in developing economies roughly double that of developed economies between 2016 and 2023.

## Core Issue

The central question is whether these new technologies and buying/selling methods genuinely transform consumers' lives, and if so, whether that transformation is mainly positive. The user's position is that it is transformative and, on balance, beneficial — mainly through greater choice for buyers and new opportunities for sellers.

## Viewpoint 1 — Wider Consumer Choice Through Global Access

Stance:
Advantage

Source basis:
User-provided material

Core idea:
Technology lets consumers buy goods — including medication — from other countries, not just what is available locally.

Explanation:
Before online ordering, buying a specialised product such as a specific medication often meant relying on a long, indirect procedure to get it from abroad, if it was possible at all. Today, a consumer can order directly from a nearby pharmacy or an online supplier, cutting out that difficulty and getting access to treatments that were previously hard to reach.

Example or support:
In the past, consumers could often only buy painkillers available in their own country, which might be less effective and could mean enduring more pain unnecessarily. Today, they can buy more effective painkillers from abroad, directly improving their quality of life.

Useful IELTS language:

- "improves your quality of life"
- "a long procedure from abroad"
- "much more effective"

Natural IELTS sentence:
Being able to order specialised products such as medication from abroad, rather than relying on a lengthy procedure, has genuinely improved many consumers' quality of life.

## Viewpoint 2 — Anyone Can Become a Seller

Advantage

Both

New technology has not only changed how people buy — it has changed who can sell. Individuals and small businesses can now reach customers directly online, including across borders.

Explanation:
Online marketplaces and social-media platforms match sellers with buyers at a scale that was not possible through a physical shop or an individual website alone. According to the OECD, this matching function lets sellers profitably offer goods and services that would previously have been impossible to sell offline. UNCTAD data also shows social-media-based commerce expanding quickly, giving ordinary individuals — not just large retailers — a route to selling.

## Viewpoint 3 — Convenience and Time Saved

Advantage

Buying no longer requires travelling to one or several shops; a purchase can be completed in moments from home.

Explanation:
The user's own introduction highlights that consumers can "buy whatever we want, just by clicking a button on a smart phone," compared with the earlier need to visit or drive to multiple shops. This saves time and effort that used to be a normal part of shopping.

Example or support:

Natural IELTS sentence:

## Viewpoint 4 — Consumer Protection Risks Remain a Limitation

Stance:
Disadvantage

Source basis:
External source

Core idea:
The same online marketplaces that empower new sellers also create consumer protection challenges, such as unreliable sellers and cross-border disputes.

Explanation:
The OECD notes that while online marketplaces expand who can sell and what can be sold, this growth brings real risks: buyers may deal with sellers they cannot easily verify, and disputes across borders can be harder to resolve than with a local shop.

Example or support:
No specific example was supplied in the user's material; this viewpoint is drawn from the OECD source only.

Useful IELTS language:

- "consumer protection challenges"
- "sellers they cannot easily verify"
- "harder to resolve across borders"

Natural IELTS sentence:
At the same time, the same platforms that empower small sellers can make it harder for consumers to verify who they are actually buying from.

## Weighing the Arguments

The strongest support in the user's own material is Viewpoint 1 (medication access), because it is developed with a clear mechanism (bypassing a long import procedure) and a concrete example (painkillers). Viewpoint 3 (convenience) is well supported by the user's introduction but stays general, without a specific example. Viewpoint 2 (new sellers) is currently only a labelled reason without explanation or example from the user, so external sources were needed to support it; the OECD and UNCTAD material confirm the mechanism (platforms matching buyers and sellers, rapid growth of social commerce) but not a personal example. Viewpoint 4 is a genuine limitation drawn only from external research — it does not overturn the case that these changes are transformative, but it shows the transformation is not without cost, which the essay could acknowledge briefly without weakening its overall position.

## Final Position

Overall, I would argue that new technologies and new ways of buying and selling have strongly transformed consumers' lives, mainly by giving people far greater choice (including access to products like medication from abroad) and by turning ordinary individuals into potential sellers on a global scale, even though some real consumer-protection risks remain part of that transformation.

## Useful Vocabulary and Expressions

### "transformative"

Meaning:
Causing a major, fundamental change, not just a small improvement.

Example sentence:
The shift from shop-based buying to smartphone ordering has been transformative for how consumers access goods.

### "a long procedure from abroad"

Meaning:
A slow, indirect process previously needed to obtain something not available locally.

Example sentence:
Consumers used to depend on a long procedure from abroad just to get a specific medication.

### "reach customers directly"

Meaning:
Sellers connecting with buyers without needing a traditional shop or middleman.

Example sentence:
Online platforms let individual sellers reach customers directly, without opening a physical shop.

## Discussion Recap

1. The main benefit is far greater consumer choice, including access to products like medication from abroad.
2. Another possible benefit is that ordinary individuals can now become sellers, reaching customers directly through online platforms.
3. The main drawback is that consumer protection remains harder to guarantee, since buyers may not be able to verify unfamiliar sellers.
4. A further concern is that cross-border disputes can be more difficult to resolve than issues with a local shop.
5. Overall, the stronger view is that these changes are genuinely transformative and, on balance, beneficial for consumers.
