## Sentence Pattern

`As Well As Being [X], I Would Argue That [Y] (body-paragraph transition)`

### Function

Move from Body 1 to Body 2 without a mechanical "firstly/secondly" — signals that a
second, distinct point is being added to a point already made.

### Structure

`As well as being [adjective describing Body 1's point], I would argue that [Body 2's new point].`

### Usage

Use as the opening sentence of Body 2 when Body 1 and Body 2 are two independent
reasons for the same position (not two sides of a debate). Keeps cohesion natural
instead of relying on numbered linking words.

### Example

As well as being impractical, I would argue that training courses for parents are
unnecessary.

### IELTS Topics

- Any Agree/Disagree essay with the "Two Reasons" structure
