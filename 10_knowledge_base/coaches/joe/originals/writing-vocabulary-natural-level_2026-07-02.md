# Joe — Writing Course — Vocabulary Section: Use Your Natural Level

> Source: Joe's video course (verbatim transcript)
> Date added: 2026-07-02
> Part of: same Vocabulary section as `writing-vocabulary-intro_2026-07-02.md` — this is the conclusion of that section, and it circles back to natural-level communication rather than teaching advanced words.

---

## Transcript (verbatim)

Don't try and communicate above your natural level because this leads to lots of cohesion problems.

Now. It's not good for your score in the exam. If your writing is very disjointed, that there's certain pieces of complex language and sentences in certain places in your writing, your writing has to be consistent, um, in order to get a higher score. So make sure you always communicate at your natural level.

Um, there are many examples in great literature that use very simple vocabulary to communicate. George Orwell's Animal Farm is a good example. Native speakers only use very complex vocabulary when they are seeking to communicate a very exact meaning about something complicated. You don't try and copy them in the exam, just communicate your natural level with the words that you know. Um, that will increase your score a lot, because your writing will be a lot more coherent and there will be fewer mistakes and it will be much easier to read.

So the final thing to say is use vocabulary that is natural. Do not attempt to upgrade. For all the reasons I've been talking about so far in the course.

## Note

This is the actual takeaway of Joe's "Vocabulary" section — despite opening with "learning more vocabulary can improve your score" (`writing-vocabulary-intro_2026-07-02.md`), the section concludes with the opposite emphasis: use your natural level, don't upgrade. Consistent with Mistake #1 (Forcing Vocabulary) from earlier in the course and the live-lesson refinement (natural, correctly-known advanced words are fine; forced/memorised ones are not).
