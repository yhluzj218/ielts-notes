# V002 — Vocabulary: Describing People (Part 2)

## Core Rule

Part 2 often asks you to describe a person you know or admire. Use specific adjectives — not vague ones like `nice` or `good`.

## Positive Adjectives

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `easygoing` | relaxed, doesn't get stressed easily | "She's very easygoing — nothing seems to bother her." |
| `generous` | gives freely (time, money, help) | "He's incredibly generous — always the first to help." |
| `creative` | generates original ideas | "She's very creative — she always finds unusual solutions." |
| `talented` | naturally skilled at something | "He's a talented musician — self-taught but plays beautifully." |
| `diligent` | hardworking, careful, thorough | "She's diligent about everything she does." |
| `moral` | strong ethical principles | "He's a very moral person — he wouldn't bend the rules for anyone." |
| `extrovert` | energised by social situations | "She's very extroverted — she loves meeting new people." |
| `active` | physically or socially energetic | "He's incredibly active — always organising events." |

## Negative Adjectives (for contrast or balance)

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `arrogant` | thinks they are better than others |
| `selfish` | only considers their own needs |
| `manipulative` | controls others through indirect means |
| `stingy` | unwilling to spend or give |
| `bully` (noun) | person who intimidates others |

**Note:** `bully` = noun/verb (the person or action). `Bullish` = financial term (expecting prices to rise). Do not confuse.

## Reusable patterns

```
"One thing I've always admired about [name] is that they're [adjective]."
"What really stands out about [name] is their [adjective] nature."
"She/He is the kind of person who [characteristic behaviour]."
```
