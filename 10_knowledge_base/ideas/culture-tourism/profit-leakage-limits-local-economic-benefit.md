# Idea: Profit leakage limits local economic benefit

## Theme
culture-tourism

## Learning Status
New

## Core Idea
A place may appear to earn money from an activity, but much of the profit is actually
taken out by outside big businesses or multinational corporations, so it never really
benefits the local economy.

## Skeleton

| Step | Content |
|---|---|
| Claim | Money spent by an outside group is seldom retained within the local economy |
| Reason | Revenue and local benefit are not the same thing — what matters is who captures the spending, not how much is spent |
| Mechanism | If the businesses capturing most of the spending are foreign-owned or don't reinvest locally, the money "leaks out" rather than circulating within the local economy |
| Impact | The local community sees little real economic benefit |
| Example | Money spent by tourists is often captured by large international hotel chains and tour operators rather than staying with local shops and workers, so a small number of big businesses profit while most local workers earn low wages |

## Reusable IELTS Angle
Key rebuttal to "this activity makes the local area rich" — often paired with the
job-creation-quality argument to build a stronger economic-downside body paragraph.

## Speaking Use
Part 3 answers to "Does [tourism/foreign investment] benefit the local economy?":
"Not always as much as people assume — a lot of the money ends up going to big
international companies rather than staying in the local community."
