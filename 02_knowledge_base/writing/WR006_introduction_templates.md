# WR006 — Introduction Templates by Question Type

## Core Rule

Every introduction must:
1. **Paraphrase** the topic (do not copy the question)
2. **Signal what the essay will do** (state position OR announce structure)

Never change the strength of keywords when paraphrasing. If the question uses `nobody` or `all`, don't soften to `less` or `some` — this changes the argument.

## Templates by Type

### Agree / Disagree

> `[Paraphrase topic]. This essay will argue that [position + brief reason].`

**Example:**
> "While some argue that family businesses offer unique advantages, this essay will argue that the drawbacks tend to outweigh the benefits for most families."

---

### Discuss Both Views + Opinion

> `[Paraphrase view 1] while others believe [paraphrase view 2]. This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my own view.`

---

### Problem + Solution

> `[Paraphrase problem]. This essay will explain the main reasons behind this trend and suggest several measures to address it.`

**Example:**
> "Excessive clothing consumption has become a significant issue in modern societies. This essay will explain the key causes of this behaviour and propose effective ways to reduce it."

**What NOT to write:**
> ~~"I will show some reasons and solutions in the following paragraph."~~ (too vague — does not confirm both questions are answered)

---

### Two-Part Question

> `[Paraphrase topic]. This essay will address [first question] and then consider [second question].`

---

## Anti-patterns (avoid for ALL types)

❌ `In my opinion, I think...` (redundant — pick one)
❌ `Nowadays, more and more people...` (clichéd opening)
❌ `This is a very important topic...` (empty filler)
❌ Repeating the question verbatim
