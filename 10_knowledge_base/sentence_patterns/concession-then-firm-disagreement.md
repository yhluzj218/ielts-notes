## Sentence Pattern

`Concession + Firm Disagreement (opening)`

### Function

Open an Agree/Disagree essay by conceding that the question's premise has some
truth, then clearly stating disagreement with its conclusion — directly answers "to
what extent" questions that embed a premise ("Because of this, ...").

### Structure

`It is true that [premise from the question]. However, I completely disagree with the idea that [the question's proposed conclusion].`

### Usage

Use in the introduction whenever the question has a "because of this" or similar
premise-then-conclusion structure. Concede the premise honestly (don't weaken it),
then pivot cleanly to your own position on the conclusion.

### Example

It is true that parents shoulder a huge responsibility. However, I completely
disagree with the idea that we should therefore force all mothers and fathers to
attend parenting courses.

### IELTS Topics

- Any Agree/Disagree or "to what extent" question with an embedded premise
