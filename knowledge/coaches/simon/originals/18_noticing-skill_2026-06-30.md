# Advice: The 'noticing' skill

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

'Noticing' is one of the most important skills for any language learner.
There are so many things that you could notice in every lesson here on the member site, or when you read or listen to anything in English.
For example, in yesterday's lesson I asked you to notice the verb tenses in one of my task 1 descriptions. In other lessons, I might ask you to notice useful vocabulary, paragraph structures, coherence, cohesion, development of ideas etc.
But please don't think that noticing is easy or obvious! Most people read things too quickly and miss the little learning opportunities.
The best learners keep their eyes and ears open for these opportunities. They are keen and curious, and they gradually improve as a result.
So, are you good at noticing? What have you noticed this week?
