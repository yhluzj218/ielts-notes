# Simon Knowledge - Writing Task 2 Addendum

## Topic: Paragraph Building

### Core Principle
Each sentence in a body paragraph must have a specific job.

A strong paragraph is built by giving each sentence a different function and arranging those functions in a logical order.

---

## Five-Sentence Paragraph Method

Default body paragraph structure:

1. Topic sentence / central idea
2. Explanation
3. Specific support / reason / example
4. Result / consequence / development
5. Final sentence that completes or extends the argument

The exact sentence jobs can vary, but every sentence must add something new.

---

## Sentence Jobs Rule

Every sentence should do one clear job.

Possible sentence jobs:

- introduce the main idea
- explain the idea
- give a reason
- give an example
- describe a result
- make a prediction
- add a specific detail
- complete the argument

Avoid writing two sentences that do the same job.

---

## No-Repetition Rule

Do not repeat the same explanation in different words.

Each sentence must move the paragraph forward.

Bad pattern:

Opinion  
→ explanation  
→ same explanation again

Good pattern:

Opinion  
→ explanation  
→ example  
→ result  
→ final development

---

## Topic: Idea Development

### Core Principle
Band 7+ writing requires ideas that are presented, developed, extended, and supported.

A paragraph with one clear idea and detailed development is usually stronger than a paragraph with several undeveloped ideas.

---

## Idea Development Rules

Do not:

- move to a different idea too quickly
- repeat the same point
- write general statements only

Do:

- explain the same idea in more detail
- add something new about the same idea
- make the idea more specific
- support the idea with reasons, examples, results, or opinions

---

## Development Tools

Use one or more of the following:

- reason
- example
- result
- consequence
- personal or general opinion
- specific detail

---

## Topic: Essay Skeleton

### Core Principle
The essay skeleton holds the essay together and makes the essay coherent.

The essay skeleton contains four parts:

1. Introduction
2. First topic sentence of body paragraph 1
3. First topic sentence of body paragraph 2
4. Conclusion

---

## Essay Skeleton Practice

Before writing full essays, practise writing essay skeletons.

Purpose:

- clarify the position
- test whether the structure works
- improve coherence
- reduce planning difficulty
- prevent irrelevant body paragraphs

---

## Topic: Best Essay Structure

### Core Principle
Choose the essay structure that best answers the exact question.

Do not automatically organize essays by surface-level topic categories.

Example question:

Music and other arts seem to be features of every society.  
In what ways are music and other arts important for societies and for individuals?

Possible structures:

1. music vs other arts
2. societies vs individuals
3. way 1 vs way 2

The best structure depends on the question focus.

---

## Structure Selection Rule

Before writing, compare possible structures.

Choose the structure that:

- directly answers the question
- supports a clear position
- avoids repetition
- allows balanced paragraphs
- makes the essay easy to follow

---

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- writing body paragraphs without clear sentence jobs
- repeating the same explanation
- adding many undeveloped ideas
- choosing a structure based only on keywords
- using fixed templates without checking the question
- writing a full essay before testing the skeleton
