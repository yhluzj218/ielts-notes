# Idea: Access to goods and services has become democratised

## Theme
technology-ai

## Learning Status
New

## Core Idea
An activity, resource, or experience that used to be the preserve of a small, usually
wealthy, minority has become accessible to ordinary people — typically because costs
have fallen or technology has advanced.

## Skeleton

| Step | Content |
|---|---|
| Claim | X used to be the preserve of a privileged group |
| Reason | When the barrier to something is cost or availability, removing that barrier automatically expands who can take part |
| Mechanism | Cheaper production, mass technology, or new infrastructure removes the barrier — a general "then vs now" causal pattern: barrier existed → barrier removed → access widened |
| Impact | Increased equality / awareness / opportunity |
| Example | International travel used to be reserved for the wealthy, who could afford the time and cost of long journeys. Budget airlines and online booking have made travel affordable for the middle class, so tourism has become a mass activity rather than an elite one |

## Reusable IELTS Angle
Works as a positive ("advantage" or "then vs now") opening argument for any question
with a historical-contrast angle — especially useful for Joe's "then vs now"
introduction technique instead of pure paraphrase.

## Speaking Use
Part 3 opinion answers to "Why do you think X has become more common/popular?":
"I think it's mainly because it used to be quite expensive or exclusive, but thanks to
[cheaper technology / mass production / the internet], ordinary people can now access
it just as easily."

## Useful Chunks
- "used to be the preserve of..."
- "now within reach of ordinary people"
- "thanks to falling costs / advances in technology"
