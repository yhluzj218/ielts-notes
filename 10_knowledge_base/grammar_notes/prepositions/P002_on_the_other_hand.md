# P002 — `on the other hand` (Not `in another hand`)

## Core Rule

`on the other hand` is a **fixed phrase** meaning "in contrast" or "considering the other side."

`in another hand` does not exist in English. It is a word-for-word translation from Chinese (另一方面 → ~~in another hand~~).

**Always:** `on the other hand`

## Why it matters in IELTS

This fixed phrase is frequently used in Task 2 essays when presenting both sides of an argument. Using the wrong form signals direct Chinese-to-English translation and affects LR and GRA.

## Common mistakes

❌ `In another hand, there are also advantages.`
❌ `In the other hand, some people disagree.`
❌ `On another hand, the cost is significant.`

## Good examples

✅ `On the other hand, there are also significant advantages.`
✅ `On the other hand, some people argue that tradition should be preserved.`
✅ `On the other hand, the environmental impact cannot be ignored.`

**Similar contrast expressions (all correct):**
- `In contrast, ...`
- `However, ...`
- `Conversely, ...`
- `Nevertheless, ...`

## Reusable patterns

```
[First view]. On the other hand, [contrasting view].
[First view]. In contrast, [contrasting view].
```

**Usage note:** `on the other hand` works best when BOTH sides have already been introduced or when two perspectives are being compared directly.

## Mini practice

Correct the phrases:

1. `In another hand, technology can also have negative effects.`
2. `The policy reduces crime. In the other hand, it may restrict freedom.`
3. `Some people prefer city life. On another hand, others value the peace of the countryside.`

**Answers:**
1. **On the other hand,** technology can also have negative effects.
2. The policy reduces crime. **On the other hand,** it may restrict freedom.
3. Some people prefer city life. **On the other hand,** others value the peace of the countryside.
