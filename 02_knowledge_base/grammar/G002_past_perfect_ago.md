# G002 — `ago` Forces Simple Past, Never Past Perfect

## Core Rule

`ago` measures backward from **now**. It is a past time marker — it specifies a finished point in time.

**Rule:** `ago` → simple past only. Never use present perfect or past perfect with `ago`.

> Past perfect (`had + past participle`) is for actions **earlier than another past action**. It has nothing to do with `ago`.

## Why it matters in IELTS

This error appears in speaking, where past storytelling is common (Part 2 topics about memories, places, people). Wrong: `He had built it 40 years ago`. Right: `He built it 40 years ago`.

## Common mistakes

❌ `He has built it 40 years ago.`
❌ `She had moved here three years ago.` (past perfect with `ago` — incorrect)
❌ `They have finished the project two months ago.`

## Good examples

✅ `He built it 40 years ago.`
✅ `She moved here three years ago.`
✅ `They finished the project two months ago.`
✅ `I met her a long time ago.`

When past perfect IS correct (without `ago`):
✅ `By the time I arrived, she had already left.` (earlier than another past event)
✅ `He had finished the report before the meeting started.`

## Reusable patterns

```
[Subject] + [simple past verb] + [time] + ago.
He built it 40 years ago.
She left five minutes ago.
```

## Mini practice

Fix these:

1. `My family has moved to this city ten years ago.`
2. `She had graduated from university three years ago.`
3. `The school has been built 20 years ago.`

**Answers:**
1. My family **moved** to this city ten years ago.
2. She **graduated** from university three years ago.
3. The school **was built** 20 years ago.
