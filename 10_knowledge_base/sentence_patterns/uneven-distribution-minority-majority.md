## Sentence Pattern

[Resource or benefit] is unevenly distributed, with a small number of [group]
[receiving most], while the majority [receive less].

### Function

Contrast a small group that captures most of a benefit with the larger group that
receives little.

### Structure

`[Resource/benefit] + is unevenly distributed, with + a small number of [group] +
[benefiting], while + the majority + [receive less].`

### Usage

Use this pattern to develop an inequality argument after stating that an industry,
policy, or economic activity generates benefits. Replace both result clauses with
specific evidence of how the two groups are affected.

Use `while` to make the contrast between the minority and majority clear. Ensure
that `the majority` refers to an identifiable population.

### Example

The benefits of automation are unevenly distributed, with a small number of
technology companies earning enormous profits, while the majority of displaced
workers receive little support.
