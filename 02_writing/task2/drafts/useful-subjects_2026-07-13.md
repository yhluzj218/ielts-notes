# Useful Subjects vs Study What You Like — Task 2

- **日期：** 2026-07-13
- **類型：** Task 2（Discuss Both Views + Opinion）
- **用途：** Matt 週三 7/15 課堂材料（Sprint-001 Week 2 Mon）；同時是 Matt 6/26 作業 #2

## 題目

Some people think students should only study subjects that are useful, such as science and technology. Others believe students should study whatever they like. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

## 我的草稿

There are various subjects in school time. Some subjects like science and techology are regarded as "useful", while other subjects are not. Under this point of view, people believes students should only study those "useful" subjects rather than what they truly like. This essay will examine both perpectives before presentating my opinion, arguing that students should study according to their preferences.

There is no doubt that having a solid knowledge on those fundamental subjects is essential to latter study path. Image that, once you have strong capability of mathmatics, it actually help you with comprehension of physics. The concept itself are highly connected with many other knowledge. That is the main reason why science and technology are prerequisite to most other classes. The more efforts you spend on those subjects like science and techology, it reward you a competitive advantageous in a long term.

However, the dominent factor to make a person consistently study is not about how practical the subject is. The eager and passion does. A student only study subjects that is considered as essential but with no interests, is easily to burn out, and give up easily. Allowing them to study whatever they like actually turn decisions back to them. Students tends to perform better if that is under her/his willing. Once they have made an achievement, they are likely to pursue a higher goal. It results in a profound and long-lasting sense of accomplishment. That is why I agree with this statement.

To sum up, although fundamental knowledge are important and worth of studing, it should not be the only subjects to focus on. I strongly agree that letting pupils to learn and explore the subjects they love can truly bring value to them.
