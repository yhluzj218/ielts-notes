# L001 — Distractors: Wait for the Full Condition

## Core Rule

In IELTS Listening, the **first keyword you hear is often the wrong answer**. The recording introduces a wrong option first, then corrects or qualifies it.

> **Rule:** Do not write until you hear the full condition or correction.

## Why it matters in IELTS

Distractor patterns are systematic in S3 and S4. Recognising them is a learnable skill that directly prevents losing marks on questions you actually understood.

## Common distractor patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| First mention → wrong, second mention → correct | `"We could meet Monday... actually, Tuesday works better."` |
| Positive then negative | `"The hotel is affordable... but the location is inconvenient."` |
| Partial information then complete | `"The fee is $50... that's just for adults, children pay $30."` |

## Strategy

1. Before listening: mark the answer options lightly in pencil
2. While listening: hold back — let the speaker finish the thought
3. After the condition is complete: write the answer
4. If you're unsure: circle both options and decide on review
