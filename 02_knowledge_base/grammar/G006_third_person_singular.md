# G006 — Third-Person Singular: `she/he/it` + Verb-S

## Core Rule

In **present simple**, a third-person singular subject (`she`, `he`, `it`, or any singular noun) requires:
- Main verb: add `-s` or `-es`
- Auxiliary: use `has` (not `have`)

This applies in BOTH speaking and writing.

## Why it matters in IELTS

This is one of the most basic grammar rules in English, and omitting the `-s` in speaking is highly visible. It directly affects GRA in both skills.

## Common mistakes

❌ `She have never visited Japan.`
❌ `He go to work every day.`
❌ `My friend always talk too much.`
❌ `The company offer good salaries.`

## Good examples

✅ `She has never visited Japan.`
✅ `He goes to work every day.`
✅ `My friend always talks too much.`
✅ `The company offers good salaries.`

**Special cases:**
- `does` (not `do`): `He doesn't like coffee.`
- `is` (not `are`): `She is a teacher.`
- Modal verbs: NO `-s` → `She can swim.` / `He might leave.`

## Reusable patterns

**Quick check before speaking/writing:**
→ Subject = `she / he / it / name / singular noun`?
→ Tense = present simple?
→ If both yes → add `-s` to main verb / use `has`

## Mini practice

Correct the errors:

1. `My sister live in Taipei and she have two children.`
2. `The government usually decide these policies.`
3. `He often forget to bring his notebook.`

**Answers:**
1. My sister **lives** in Taipei and she **has** two children.
2. The government usually **decides** these policies.
3. He often **forgets** to bring his notebook.
