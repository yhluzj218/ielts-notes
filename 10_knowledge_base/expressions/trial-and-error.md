## Expression

`trial and error`

### Function

Describe learning or improving something by repeatedly attempting it, making
mistakes, and adjusting — a natural way to talk about how skills are acquired
without formal instruction.

### Usage

Use when arguing that a skill or ability can be learned informally/experientially,
as a contrast to formal training or education.

### Common Collocations

- learn by trial and error
- through a process of trial and error
- develop [a skill] by trial and error

### Example

Adults learn to be good parents partly by trial and error, gradually working out
what does and doesn't work with their own children.
