## 題目

Some people say that it is better to work for a large company than a small one. Do you agree or disagree?

## 我的草稿（含 Joe 眉批）

Working in a multinational company seems like a dream job to most people. Many graduates tends to find a job from well-known companies, like facebook, Microsoft or other famous cooperations, while private firms are less attractive to them. I partially agree with this opinion for the two main reasons.
> Joe：Write your opinion in the third person = "I partially agree" = This essay argues
> Joe：Write the reasons for your opinion more clearly.

First and foremost, large companies have more resources in many aspects, such as funding to research and develop, taking care of employees well-beings and more compliant with operational regulations. As employees in these companies are well protected under an organised system. Everything follows proper guidelines and predefined plans. It is unlikely not to get paid or work in an unsafe place. In addition, it is likely to offer more opportunities to explore new projects with higher budgets.
> Joe：One main idea → Explain → Example

In contrast, working in a small firm tends to have more chances to become a pioneer of a startup company. While a company is small, job boundaries are vague and allows personal contributions more visible to the organisation. Once a big success for a project or idea, you can gain more benefits with more flexible way. For example, having shares of stock or become a core member. Working in a small company is like to taking a higher risk but might gain higher rewards. And, that is all depends on your contribution to a company's profit.
> Joe：One main idea → Explain → Example

Despite the potential benefits of working in a small company, it is obvious that being an employee of large companies could gain instant and direct rewards. In general, working for a well-known company outweighs working in a small company.
> Joe：Also be careful with spelling.

### 這篇為什麼拿不到 7（補充上方 Part A 細項的理由）

- **「Overall progression」只給 6 的原因講明了**：「I couldn't give you seven for that one because you haven't written your reasons for your opinion in the introduction. Examiners really like it when your essay is consistent from beginning to middle to end.」→ Intro 沒有預告 opinion 的 reasons，導致全文一致性斷掉。
- **Body 段落是「a list of ideas」**：「You don't want to have a list of ideas like this. Any idea you give in your writing, explain it in detail and then give an example.」他點名的句子：`In addition, it is likely to offer more opportunities to explore new projects with higher budgets` ——這種 idea 丟出來就要接 explanation + example，不能再疊下一個 idea。
- 第三人稱表態（`this essay argues` 取代 `I`）+ 把 reasons 寫清楚——「You did that in the essay I just marked. So that's good. Just keep doing that.」（celebrity-privacy 已經改對了）
