# IELTS Advice: from 6.5 to 7

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

Here are three pieces of advice for people who are stuck at band 6.5.
1. Small improvements
If you are able to get a 6.5, you are very close to getting a band 7. In the writing and speaking tests, a score of 6.5 means that you are already achieving band 7 in half of the requirements.
So, you don't need a special technique or piece of advice. You only need to make small improvements. For example, in the speaking and writing tests, you could express your ideas in a bit more detail, or try to reduce the number of small mistakes that you make.
2. Don't change your methods
When students are stuck on band 6.5, they often think that they need a new technique, a new book or some new advice. This is usually wrong!
Remember: If you have a 6.5 in writing or speaking, it means that you are already getting band 7 in 50% of the scoring criteria. For example, you might be getting 7 for task response and 7 for coherence, but 6 for vocabulary and 6 for grammar (examiners can't give half marks in these criteria). So your method is working quite well and doesn't need changing.
3. Improve in just one area
You can probably raise your score by half a band by improving in just one area.
Instead of worrying about everything (grammar, vocabulary, organisation etc.), just choose one area to focus on. For example, a small improvement in the quality of your ideas (vocabulary) could lift your score from 6.5 to 7.
