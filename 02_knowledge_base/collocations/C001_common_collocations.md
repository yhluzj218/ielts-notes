# C001 — Common Collocations for Writing and Speaking

## Core Rule

A collocation is a word pair or group that sounds natural together in English. Using the wrong partner word sounds unnatural to a native speaker even if the meaning is clear.

## High-Use Collocations

### Social / Policy Topics

| Collocation | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `play a significant role in` | be an important factor in | `Social media plays a significant role in shaping opinion.` |
| `have a profound impact on` | affect deeply | `The policy had a profound impact on employment.` |
| `raise public awareness` | help society understand an issue | `Campaigns can raise public awareness about mental health.` |
| `address this issue` | deal with a problem deliberately | `Governments need to address this issue urgently.` |
| `be driven by` | be caused or motivated by | `Consumer behaviour is largely driven by marketing.` |
| `contribute to` | be one cause of (+ noun) | `Fast fashion contributes to environmental damage.` |
| `lead to` | result in | `Poor planning can lead to project failure.` |

### Argument / Opinion

| Collocation | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `in the long run` | over a long time period | `Education is more effective in the long run.` |
| `in the short term` | in the near future | `The policy may help in the short term.` |
| `to some extent` | partially | `To some extent, this is true.` |
| `tend to` | usually / generally | `People tend to buy more than they need.` |
| `as a result of` | because of (+ noun) | `As a result of the campaign, attitudes changed.` |

### Education / Work

| Collocation | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `pursue a career` | work toward a career | `She pursued a career in medicine.` |
| `gain experience` | acquire experience | `Internships help students gain experience.` |
| `develop skills` | build skills over time | `This programme helps students develop skills.` |

## Preposition reminder

`contribute TO` ✅ (not `contribution AT`)
`listen TO` ✅ (not `listen` without preposition)
`on social media` ✅ (not `in social media`)
