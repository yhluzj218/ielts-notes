# Idea: Profit leakage limits local economic benefit

## Theme
culture-tourism

## Core Idea
A place may appear to earn money from an activity, but much of the profit is actually
taken out by outside big businesses or multinational corporations, so it never really
benefits the local economy.

## Why
Revenue and local benefit are not the same thing. If the businesses capturing most of
the spending are foreign-owned or not reinvesting locally, the money spent in a place
"leaks out" rather than circulating within the local economy.

## Example
Money spent by tourists is often captured by large international hotel chains and
tour operators rather than staying with local shops and workers, so a small number of
big businesses profit while most local workers earn low wages.

## Reusable IELTS Angle
Key rebuttal to "this activity makes the local area rich" — often paired with the
job-creation-quality argument to build a stronger economic-downside body paragraph.

## Writing Use
Body paragraph chain:
`[Point] money spent by [group] is seldom retained by the local economy → [Explain] profits are taken out by [outside entity, e.g. multinational corporations] → [Example] a small number of big businesses profit while most workers earn low wages → [Result] the local community sees little real economic benefit`

## Speaking Use
Part 3 answers to "Does [tourism/foreign investment] benefit the local economy?":
"Not always as much as people assume — a lot of the money ends up going to big
international companies rather than staying in the local community."

## Useful Chunks
- "seldom retained by the local economy"
- "profits are taken out by..."
- "little real economic benefit"
