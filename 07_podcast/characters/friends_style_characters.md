# Characters — Friends-Style Circle (café gathering crew)

A small recurring friend group for café/apartment scenes — warm banter, relatable
life complaints, not plot-heavy. Use 1-2 of these per episode, not all at once.

## Léa

French, Connie's closest friend outside work. Dry sense of humour, very direct,
comfortable telling Connie when she's overthinking something. Good source of
natural concession + blunt-opinion language.

## Marcus

Laid-back, works in something creative (photography/art), perpetually between
projects. The "2 Broke Girls" energy — broke, funny about it, quick with a
comeback. Good for informal collocations and complaint-register language
("I end up spending way too much on...", "it's not exactly cheap, but...").

## Priya

Practical, slightly exasperated by everyone else's drama, usually the one who
brings the conversation back to what actually needs to get done. Good for
problem/solution and "a more balanced approach would be to..." style language.

Add more characters here as episodes need them — keep each sketch short (role,
personality, voice, what kind of language they naturally produce).
