## Sentence Pattern

[X] now [verb phrase] more or less [Y] than was the case [time period] ago.

### Function

Compare a present situation with the equivalent situation in the past without
repeating the full earlier clause.

### Structure

`[Present situation] + than was the case + [time period] ago.`

### Usage

Use this pattern to describe change across time in formal writing. `Was the case`
refers back to the situation already described and avoids repetition.

Use `is the case` when comparing two groups or places in the present. Use `was the
case` when the comparison is with the past.

### Example

Employees now change careers more frequently than was the case a generation ago.
