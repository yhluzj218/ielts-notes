# SP003 — Natural Delivery: Simple Language Beats Complex Vocabulary

## Core Rule

**Fluency > Complexity.** A natural, clear sentence scores higher than a complex sentence that sounds rehearsed or causes hesitation.

> "Keep it natural, keep it simple — for Band 7, this is completely sufficient." — Joe

## Why it matters in IELTS

The Fluency & Coherence criterion rewards natural speech flow. Hesitation caused by searching for a "more impressive" word actively lowers the score. Band 8+ requires only 3–4 complex sentences throughout the whole exam — not constant complexity.

## Connected speech (Matt's rule)

English speakers do NOT pronounce every word separately with full clarity.

| Written | Natural speech |
|---|---|
| `I would like to go` | `I'd like to go` |
| `want to` | `wanna` (informal) |
| `going to` | `gonna` (informal) |
| `I am` | `I'm` |
| `She has` | `She's` |

**The problem:** Pronouncing every word "too correctly" sounds artificial and reduces naturalness score.

**Practice:** Use contractions (`I'd / She's / We've / They're`) as the default in speaking.

## What to avoid (Joe's explicit rules)

- **No imitation of native speakers** — your communication style is already good; copying creates interference
- **No YouTube / social media IELTS tips** — examiners know those templates and penalise them
- **No memorised scripts** — examiners deliberately ask unexpected questions to test flexibility
- **No overthinking** — the time you spend searching for the "perfect word" lowers fluency more than the word would have helped

## High-score principle

> "Convey a large amount of natural information and detail — not complex language."

Simple language that communicates clearly = better score than complex language that sounds rehearsed.
