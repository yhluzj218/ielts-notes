# Sentence Pattern

`This essay will examine both perspectives and argue that [position]`

### Function

Thesis statement for a Discuss Both Views + Opinion essay: signals that both views
will be discussed AND states the writer's position upfront, in third person.

### Structure

Two main frames (both fill the same slot — pick one per essay):

1. **Direct:** `This essay will examine both perspectives and argue that [your position as a full clause].`
2. **Concessive:** `While both [options/arrangements/approaches] have clear advantages, this essay will argue that [position].`
   - `While` 開頭 = 先承認雙方都有理，逗號後亮自己立場（while 子句後必須接對立面 + 逗號 — Matt 6/26）
   - 讓步版比直接版多做一件事：預告 Body 1 會公平對待弱方

Minimal variation:
- `This essay will discuss both points of view before giving my own opinion.`（只預告，不先表態）

### Usage

Second sentence of the introduction (sentence 1 = paraphrase of the two views,
sentence 2 = this thesis; intro = 2 sentences total). Third-person framing
("This essay argues") preferred over "I partially agree" — Joe's official feedback
2026-07-11. Stating the position upfront also anchors Body 2 and prevents topic
drift.

### Example

- Direct: This essay will examine both perspectives and argue that city living
  offers more overall benefits.
- Concessive: While both arrangements have clear advantages, this essay will argue
  that working from home is the better option for most employees.

### IELTS Topics

- Any Discuss Both Views + Opinion question (education, work, lifestyle, technology)
