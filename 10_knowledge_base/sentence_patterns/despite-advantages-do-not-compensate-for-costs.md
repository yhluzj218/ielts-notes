## Sentence Pattern

In my view, despite [advantages], these do not compensate for [costs or damage].

### Function

Give a final judgement that acknowledged benefits are outweighed by more serious
disadvantages.

### Structure

`In my view, despite + noun phrase describing advantages, these do not compensate
for + noun phrase describing costs or damage.`

### Usage

Use this pattern in the conclusion of an advantages–disadvantages essay when your
position is that the disadvantages are greater.

`Despite` must be followed by a noun phrase, not a complete clause. The pronoun
`these` must clearly refer to plural advantages already mentioned. If the reference
could be unclear, repeat `these benefits` instead.

### Example

In my view, despite the convenience offered by private cars, these benefits do not
compensate for the resulting congestion and air pollution.
