## 切題與結構分析

**題型判斷：** `Discuss both these views and give your own opinion` → Discuss Both Views，你判斷正確，Intro 也正確預告了兩個觀點 + 自己的立場。

**Body 1 有輕微偏題：** 題目問的是「被專業攝影師到處跟拍」這件事好不好，但你 Body 1 的論點實際上是「名人透過自己的作品/故事讓大眾認識真實的自己」（Michael Jackson 的例子），這其實比較接近「名人自願分享」而不是「被攝影師跟拍」。這跟你 Intro 寫的「supposed to share their lives to people」呼應，但已經悄悄把題目的「攝影師主動跟拍」改寫成「名人主動分享」——依你的 Task 2 checklist：「改寫題目時有沒有改掉原意？」這裡稍微改掉了。這不是嚴重問題（考官還是看得出你在回答這題），但拉低了 TR 分數的空間。

**Body 1 的具體修法：** 把例子改成「攝影師跟拍」直接帶來的好處，例如：狗仔拍到的隨性生活照，反而讓粉絲覺得偶像更真實、更貼近人（而不是包裝過的官方照片/訪談）——這樣才是真正在論證「被跟拍」這件事本身的好處，不是「名人自己選擇分享」的好處。

**Body 2 執行得很好：** Diana 的例子具體、有 Point→Explain→Example→Result 完整鏈，結尾有清楚表態句（"That is exactly why I think..."），份量也比 Body 1 扎實。這是這篇最大的進步。

**段落內部問題（Body 1）：**
- `Not only a packaged idol, but with more solid picture of who he is.` 是一個片段句，沒有主詞+動詞，讀起來像是上一句的延續但斷開了。
- 代名詞混亂：先用 "him"/"his"（指 Michael Jackson，單數），下一句突然跳到 "their works"（複數，指名人整體）——讀者會困惑指的是誰。

**Conclusion 的文法問題：** `Despite revealing private lives to fans does make...` — `despite` 後面接的應該是名詞/動名詞片語，不能直接接「主詞+動詞」的完整子句。這句話文法上是錯的，不是連接詞誤用（不是 Although+However 那種雙重連接詞問題），是 `despite` 的用法本身錯了。

## Band 7.5 改寫版（依老師建議：第三人稱表態、每段 Point→Explain→Example，盡量保留妳的原意）

Celebrities are constantly in the public eye, and few topics divide opinion more than the practice of professional photographers following them wherever they go. Some argue that this is acceptable because the public has a genuine interest in celebrities' lives, while others believe that photographers are wrong to pursue famous people so relentlessly. This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my own view, arguing that the paparazzi's pursuit of celebrities ultimately causes more harm than good.

Those who support this practice point out that candid photography can reveal a more authentic side of a celebrity than any carefully managed interview or performance ever could. Michael Jackson, for instance, was presented on stage as a flawless, larger-than-life idol, yet unposed photographs of him quietly visiting children's hospitals showed a gentler, more human side of his character. Fans who see this unfiltered version of a celebrity's life often feel a stronger sense of connection to them, since it suggests the public persona is not entirely manufactured. This, in turn, can make a celebrity's work resonate more deeply with audiences, who feel they are engaging with a real person rather than a polished brand.

However, being watched and photographed at every moment places an immense psychological burden on individuals, one that can ultimately push them toward tragedy. Princess Diana is perhaps the clearest example of this danger. She was pursued so persistently by photographers that even a routine journey became an attempt to escape their cameras, and this pursuit ended in the car accident that killed her. Her death left a lasting impression on the public precisely because it exposed how far unchecked photography could go in endangering a person's life, not merely their comfort. This is exactly why I believe that photographers are wrong to follow celebrities with such intensity.

While it is true that revealing glimpses of a celebrity's private life can make them appear more relatable and can strengthen the public's connection to their work, this benefit does not outweigh the very real cost to their safety and mental wellbeing. The pressure created by constant surveillance is long-lasting and can lead to consequences far more serious than any benefit gained from a moment of authenticity.
