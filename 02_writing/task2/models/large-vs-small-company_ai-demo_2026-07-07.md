## 題目

Some people say that it is better to work for a large company than a small one. Do you agree or disagree?

## 用的 Skeleton

Variation A — Two Reasons（`10_knowledge_base/writing/skeleton_agree_disagree.md`）

```
Intro:  情境切入 + 立場（不是純改寫題目）
Body 1: 理由一（資源/訓練）+ Point→Explain→Example→Result
Body 2: 理由二（穩定性）+ Point→Explain→Example→Result
Concl:  重申立場，加一句讓步（承認對方優點，但不推翻立場）
```

## 為什麼這樣寫是「答對」這題

- 立場一開始就講清楚，不是列優缺點
- 兩個理由完全不重疊：一個講「公司能給你什麼」（訓練），一個講「公司能不能撐住」（穩定性）
- 每段都有具體例子，不是抽象敘述
- 結論有讓步但沒推翻立場——是「acknowledge, not agree」，跟单純列優缺點不同

## 範文全文

Choosing between a large organisation and a small business is one of the most common dilemmas job seekers face, and the two options come with genuinely different trade-offs. In my view, working for a large company is generally the better choice, mainly because of the training such companies can offer and the stability they provide during difficult economic times.

To begin with, large companies are usually able to invest far more in developing their staff than small firms can. Because they have bigger budgets and more established systems, multinational companies can afford to run structured training programmes, pay for professional certifications, and assign new employees a mentor to guide them through their first few years. A friend of mine, for example, joined a large accounting firm straight after university and was immediately enrolled in a two-year training scheme that paid for her professional exams — something a small local accountancy practice simply would not have had the budget to offer. As a result, employees at large companies often develop their skills faster and reach a professional qualification much sooner than they would in a smaller organisation.

Equally important is the greater job security that large companies tend to offer. Because they operate in multiple markets and often have several income streams, large corporations are usually better able to absorb a downturn in one part of their business without laying off staff. Small companies, in contrast, often depend on a single product, service, or client, so when that one source of income disappears, the whole business — and every job attached to it — can disappear with it. During the most recent economic slowdown, for instance, several small shops on my street closed within months, while employees at the large supermarket chain nearby kept their jobs throughout. This kind of stability matters a great deal to anyone who is planning their finances or supporting a family.

Of course, working for a small company does have its own appeal — employees are often more visible and can take on responsibility more quickly. However, this benefit is outweighed by the training and stability that large companies can provide, which is why I believe that, for most people, working for a large company is the better option.

## 結構拆解

| 段落 | 功能 | 這篇怎麼做 |
|---|---|---|
| Intro | 情境切入 + 立場 | 不是改寫題目，是先講「這是一個真實的兩難」再表態 |
| Body 1 | 理由一：訓練資源 | Point（投資員工）→ Explain（預算/制度）→ Example（朋友的具體經歷）→ Result（技能發展更快） |
| Body 2 | 理由二：穩定性 | Point（多元市場更抗風險）→ Explain（單一收入來源的小公司脆弱）→ Example（街上小店倒閉 vs 超市連鎖撐過去）→ Result（財務規劃的重要性） |
| Concl | 重申立場 + 讓步 | 承認小公司的能見度優點，但說明沒推翻結論 |

## 好的應用單字/片語

- `come with genuinely different trade-offs` — 開場句型，講兩個選項各有優劣時很好用
- `structured training programmes` / `assign a mentor` — 講公司資源時的自然搭配
- `absorb a downturn` — 講「撐過景氣衰退」的自然說法，比 survive 更精準
- `depend on a single product/service/client` — 講小公司脆弱性的自然句型
- `this benefit is outweighed by [X]` — 結論讓步句，承認對方優點但守住立場

## 連接詞（沒有用 firstly/secondly）

- `To begin with, ...` — Body 1 開頭
- `Equally important is ...` — Body 2 開頭，自然承接，不是套公式
