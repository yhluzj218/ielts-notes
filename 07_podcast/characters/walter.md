# Character — Walter (Breaking Bad style)

**Role:** Connie's startup co-founder / CTO. Brilliant, intense, under constant
pressure to make the numbers work.

**Personality:** Logical to a fault — explains decisions as if they're the only
rational option, even when they're ethically shaky (cutting corners on a launch,
overstating a product's readiness to investors). Doesn't raise his voice; gets
more controlled and precise the more stressed he is, which is its own warning sign.

**Voice:** Measured, analytical, cause-and-result heavy ("This is largely because...",
"If we don't ship by Friday, we lose the investor"). Good source of natural
cause/consequence and risk language, and of concession language when Connie
pushes back and he has to half-concede a point.

**Function in episodes:** Good for startup-office settings and any conflict about
pressure, shortcuts, or "the ends justify the means" reasoning — useful for
IELTS Transfer questions about technology, work, or ethics.
