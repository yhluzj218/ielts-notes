# Idea: No long-term stake leads to irresponsible behaviour

## Theme
government-society

## Learning Status
New

## Core Idea
When a person only participates in a place or system briefly, and won't experience the
long-term consequences of their actions, they tend to behave less responsibly.

## Skeleton

| Step | Content |
|---|---|
| Claim | A group that only stays/participates for a short time tends to behave less responsibly |
| Reason | Responsibility is usually reinforced by having to live with the outcome |
| Mechanism | Remove that future consequence — because you're leaving soon, or won't be personally affected — and the incentive to act carefully weakens; the person doesn't experience the long-term consequences of their actions |
| Impact | The cost is borne by others (residents/environment/future) |
| Example | Tourists litter or behave carelessly in a destination because they won't be the ones living with the consequences, unlike local residents |

## Reusable IELTS Angle
General causal explanation for "why does group X behave irresponsibly/badly" —
not limited to tourism, works for any short-term-participation scenario.

## Speaking Use
Part 3 answers to "Why do some people not care about the environment / their
surroundings?": "I think part of it is that if you're only somewhere temporarily, you
don't really feel the long-term impact of your actions, so there's less incentive to
be careful."

## Useful Chunks
- "don't experience the long-term consequences"
- "won't live with the result"
- "the cost is borne by others"
