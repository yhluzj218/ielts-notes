# G004 — Common Uncountable Nouns: `feedback`, `advice`, `staff`

## Core Rule

These nouns are **always uncountable** — no plural `-s`, no article `a/an`.

| Word | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---|---|---|
| `feedback` | `a feedback` / `feedbacks` | `some feedback` / `a piece of feedback` |
| `advice` | `an advice` / `advices` | `some advice` / `a piece of advice` |
| `staff` | `staffs` | `staff` / `staff members` |
| `furniture` | `a furniture` / `furnitures` | `some furniture` / `a piece of furniture` |
| `equipment` | `equipments` | `equipment` / `a piece of equipment` |
| `luggage` | `luggages` | `luggage` / `a piece of luggage` |

## Why it matters in IELTS

These words appear frequently in Task 1 letters (feedback, advice) and Task 2 essays (staff, equipment). Pluralising them signals a systematic error in GRA.

## Common mistakes

❌ `She gave me a useful feedback.`
❌ `The staffs are working hard.`
❌ `I need some advices about my career.`

## Good examples

✅ `She gave me some useful feedback.`
✅ `The staff are working hard.` (collective — takes plural verb in British English)
✅ `I need some advice about my career.`
✅ `There are 20 staff members in the department.` (when counting people)

## Reusable patterns

To express one item: `a piece of [feedback / advice / equipment / furniture / luggage]`
To express quantity: `some / a lot of / much [uncountable noun]`

## Mini practice

Correct the errors:

1. `The manager gave all employees a feedback about their performance.`
2. `How many staffs work in this office?`
3. `She offered me some advices that helped me a lot.`

**Answers:**
1. The manager gave all employees **some feedback** about their performance.
2. How many **staff members** work in this office? / How many **members of staff** work here?
3. She offered me some **advice** that helped me a lot.
