# SP001 — Part 2 Strategy: Speak for the Full 2 Minutes

## Core Rule

**The #1 rule for Part 2: speak for the full 2 minutes.**

Stopping early is worse than speaking too long. The examiner will stop you when time is up — you do not need to manage your own ending.

## Why it matters in IELTS

Part 2 is specifically designed to test whether you can sustain speech for an extended period (Fluency & Coherence). Running out of things to say is the most common reason for low scores.

## Structure (use the question card itself)

The question card always provides the structure. Use it:

```
Opening:  "What it is / who it is" — state the topic clearly
Middle:   Details from the card (where, how, when, who with)
Filler:   "Let me tell you a bit more about it..."
          "One thing I remember was when..."
          "The reason I chose this is..."
Closing:  Feelings / what it meant to you / impact
```

**Don't try to create a new structure.** The bullet points on the card are your outline.

## Preparation time (1 minute)

Use the 1 minute to:
1. Choose your **topic / example** — pick one you can talk about easily
2. Avoid topics with high vocabulary barriers (e.g., a complex film plot)
3. Simple topics with personal details outperform impressive topics with no content

**Example:** "Describe a TV show you enjoy"
→ `Big Bang Theory / Friends` (easy to describe) > `Breaking Bad` (hard to explain plot)

## What to avoid

- Connecting language / formal transitions: NOT needed in Part 2
- A conclusion: NOT needed — examiner will stop you
- Scripted answers: examiners recognise and penalise prepared answers
- Stopping at 90 seconds: always try to fill the time

## Extending when you run out of ideas

```
"One thing I particularly remember about this is..."
"What struck me most was..."
"Looking back, I think the reason I enjoyed it so much was..."
"Another detail I should mention is..."
```

## Mini practice

Try this: Set a 2-minute timer. Describe a place you have visited.
→ Stop only when the timer rings — not before.
→ If stuck: add a detail, add a feeling, say what you would do differently.
