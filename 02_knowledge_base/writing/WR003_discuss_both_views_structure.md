# WR003 — Discuss Both Views + Opinion Structure

## Core Rule

You must **genuinely argue BOTH sides** — not just mention one side dismissively.

Body 1 = the side you **don't prefer** (but argue it fairly)
Body 2 = the side you **do prefer** (your actual view)
Conclusion = your overall position

> **Matt's key principle:** In Body 1, your job is to advocate for the opposing view — even if you personally disagree. Write as if you understand and accept that position. Examiners can tell when you're dismissive.

## Essay Structure

```
Introduction
  → Paraphrase both views
  → Signal: "This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my own view."

Body 1 — THE OTHER SIDE (argue it properly)
  → State the view
  → Explain WHY people hold this view
  → Give a convincing reason or example
  → Make it sound persuasive

Body 2 — YOUR SIDE (your preferred view)
  → State your view
  → Explain why this side is stronger
  → Give a reason + example + consequence

Conclusion
  → Summarise both views briefly
  → Restate your overall position
```

## Introduction Template

> `[Paraphrase view 1] while others believe [paraphrase view 2]. This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my own view.`

## Body 1 critical rule (Matt)

Most candidates fail Body 1 by writing: `"Some people think X because Y."` — one sentence, no development.

**Required:** Even for the side you disagree with, write a full, developed paragraph:
- Why do people genuinely believe this?
- What is the strongest argument for this view?
- What example supports it?

> Analogy: Even if you disagree with a political position, you should be able to write a convincing paragraph explaining why others support it.

## Common mistakes

❌ Body 1 only 2 sentences (underdeveloped)
❌ Body 1 sounds dismissive: "some people think X, but this is wrong..."
❌ Not giving your own opinion at all
❌ Conclusion introduces a new argument
