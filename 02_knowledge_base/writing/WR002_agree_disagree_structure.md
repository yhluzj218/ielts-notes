# WR002 — Agree/Disagree Essay Structure

## Core Rule

State your position **clearly in the introduction**. Do not sit on the fence.

> Agree/Disagree = you must choose a side. The examiner is testing whether you can argue a position, not whether you can list both sides.

## Essay Structure

```
Introduction
  → Paraphrase the topic
  → State your position clearly (agree OR disagree)

Body 1
  → Strongest reason supporting your position
  → Full development: Point → Explain → Support → Result

Body 2
  → Second reason OR acknowledge opposing view and refute it
  → Full development

Conclusion
  → Restate your position in different words
  → Optional: one wider implication
```

## Introduction Template

> `[Paraphrase of topic]. This essay will argue that [your position] because [brief reason].`

**Example:**
> "While some argue that family businesses offer unique advantages, this essay will argue that the drawbacks tend to outweigh the benefits for most families."

## Position statement rules

- State position in introduction — not buried in conclusion
- Use clear language: "I strongly agree..." / "This essay will argue that..."
- Do NOT write: "There are both advantages and disadvantages..." (fence-sitting in Agree/Disagree)

## Conclusion template

> "In conclusion, [restate position in different words]. [Optional: one longer-term implication.]"

## Common mistakes

❌ Not stating a position in the introduction
❌ Treating Agree/Disagree like a Discuss Both Views essay (giving equal weight to both sides without a clear position)
❌ Adding new arguments in the conclusion
