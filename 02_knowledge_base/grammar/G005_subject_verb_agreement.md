# G005 — Subject-Verb Agreement

## Core Rule

The verb must **agree in number** with its subject.

Three common failure points:

| Pattern | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---|---|---|
| `there is/are` + noun | `There is several reasons` | `There are several reasons` |
| Plural subject + verb | `families tends to` | `families tend to` |
| Third-person singular (spoken) | `she have never` | `she has never` |

## Why it matters in IELTS

Subject-verb agreement errors affect both **Writing GRA** and **Speaking Grammatical Range**. They are the most visible grammatical signal to examiners.

## Common mistakes

❌ `There is many reasons why people prefer...`
❌ `The families in this country tends to...`
❌ `She have lived here for many years.`
❌ `Each of the students have their own opinion.`

## Good examples

✅ `There are many reasons why people prefer...`
✅ `Families in this country tend to...`
✅ `She has lived here for many years.`
✅ `Each of the students has their own opinion.` (each = singular)

**Special cases:**
- `each / every / neither / either` → singular verb
- `staff / team / government` → singular OR plural (British English allows both; be consistent)
- `number of [plural noun]` → singular: `The number of students is growing.`
- `a number of [plural noun]` → plural: `A number of students are waiting.`

## Reusable patterns

**Checking `there is/are`:**
→ Look at the noun AFTER `there is/are`
→ If plural → `there are`; if singular → `there is`

**Checking plural subjects:**
→ Strip everything between subject and verb: `The quality of the products [is/are]`
→ Subject = `quality` (singular) → `is`

## Mini practice

Choose the correct verb:

1. `There (is/are) several advantages to this approach.`
2. `The number of accidents (has/have) decreased significantly.`
3. `Each of the candidates (was/were) interviewed separately.`

**Answers:**
1. **are**
2. **has**
3. **was**
