# WR008 — Conclusion Rules

## Core Rule

A conclusion must:
1. **Restate** your position in different words
2. **Not introduce** any new arguments or points

That is all. A conclusion does NOT need to summarise every body paragraph.

## Template

> `In conclusion, [restate main position in different words]. [Optional: one wider implication or comparative point.]`

**Examples:**

Agree/Disagree:
> "In conclusion, while there are arguments on both sides, the evidence suggests that the benefits of [X] outweigh the drawbacks."

Problem+Solution:
> "In conclusion, the issue stems primarily from [causes], and can be addressed through [solutions]. While [solution A] may show quicker results, [solution B] is likely to be more effective in the long run."

## The short-term vs long-term move

Comparing solutions or effects by timeframe in the conclusion is valid and shows mature thinking:

> "While taxation may reduce consumption in the short term, education is likely to be more effective in the long run."

This is NOT a new argument — it's a comparative summary.

## Critical: Double Conjunction (G010)

❌ `Although X, however Y` — common error in conclusions when starting a contrast
✅ `Although X, Y` OR `X. However, Y.`

See [G010](../grammar/G010_double_conjunction.md) for full explanation.

## What to avoid

❌ `In conclusion, I have discussed...` (circular — you're restating what you did, not what you argued)
❌ Adding a new reason or example that wasn't in the body
❌ Changing your position in the conclusion
❌ Ending with: `I hope this essay has shown...` (sounds like a speech, not an essay)
