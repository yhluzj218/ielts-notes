# E001 — Concession Expressions

## Core Rule

Concession = acknowledging the opposing point before asserting your own.

**Critical rule (see G010):** `Although` and `However` cannot be in the same sentence. Choose one.

## Full Toolkit

### Subordinating Conjunctions (connect two clauses in one sentence)

| Expression | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `Although` | `Although [conceded], [main claim].` | Although fast fashion is cheap, it creates serious environmental problems. |
| `While` | `While [conceded], [main claim].` | While some prefer city life, others value the peace of rural areas. |
| `Even though` | `Even though [conceded], [main claim].` | Even though the cost is high, many people still buy. |
| `Despite the fact that` | `Despite the fact that [clause], [main claim].` | Despite the fact that regulations exist, enforcement is weak. |

### Adverbs (start a new sentence after a period)

| Expression | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `However,` | `[First claim]. However, [contrast].` | Education is important. However, policy changes are also needed. |
| `Nevertheless,` | `[First claim]. Nevertheless, [contrast].` | The risks are real. Nevertheless, the benefits outweigh them. |
| `That said,` | `[First claim]. That said, [contrast].` | The solution is not perfect. That said, it is a step forward. |

### Preposition Phrases (take noun phrase, not clause)

| Expression | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `Despite` | `Despite [noun phrase], [claim].` | Despite the high cost, many people still buy. |
| `In spite of` | `In spite of [noun phrase], [claim].` | In spite of government efforts, the problem persists. |

**`Despite` trap:** Takes noun phrase, NOT clause.
❌ `Despite the cost is high` → ✅ `Despite the high cost`

## Choosing the right expression

| Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| One sentence, two clauses | `Although / While / Even though` |
| Two separate sentences | `However / Nevertheless / That said` |
| Before a noun phrase | `Despite / In spite of` |
