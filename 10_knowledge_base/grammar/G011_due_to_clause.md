# G011 — `due to` Takes a Noun Phrase, Not a Clause

## Core Rule

`due to` = preposition → must be followed by a **noun phrase**
`because` = conjunction → followed by a **full clause** (subject + verb)

> **Noun phrase:** no subject, no verb
> **Clause:** has subject + verb

## Why it matters in IELTS

`due to` + clause is a very common error that mixes up a preposition with a conjunction. It affects both GRA and coherence in Writing Task 2.

## Common mistakes

❌ `due to she was late` (clause after preposition)
❌ `due to the price is too high`
❌ `due to people are more aware of...`

## Good examples

✅ `due to her lateness` (noun phrase)
✅ `due to the high price` (noun phrase)
✅ `due to increased public awareness` (noun phrase)

Using `because` for clauses:
✅ `because she was late` (clause with subject + verb)
✅ `because the price is too high`
✅ `because people are more aware of...`

## Reusable patterns

| Want to say | Use `due to` + noun | Use `because` + clause |
|---|---|---|
| reason = fact/event | `due to rising costs` | `because costs have risen` |
| reason = action | `due to government intervention` | `because the government intervened` |
| reason = adj+noun | `due to high demand` | `because demand is high` |

**`due to the fact that`** = acceptable workaround:
✅ `due to the fact that she was late` (now `that she was late` = noun clause ✓)

## Mini practice

Rewrite using the correct form:

1. `She was absent due to she was sick.`
2. `The project failed due to the team didn't communicate.`
3. `Sales dropped due to consumers lost confidence.`

**Answers:**
1. She was absent **because she was sick.** / She was absent **due to her illness.**
2. The project failed **because the team didn't communicate.** / The project failed **due to poor communication.**
3. Sales dropped **because consumers lost confidence.** / Sales dropped **due to a loss of consumer confidence.**
