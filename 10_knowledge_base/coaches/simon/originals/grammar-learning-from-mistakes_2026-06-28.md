# Learning from mistakes

Every time you make a grammar mistake or use English vocabulary in an unnatural way, you have an opportunity to learn and improve. You can also improve by learning from the mistakes that other students make.

For example, here are some sentences that people wrote below last week's lesson. They all contain mistakes or vocabulary problems:

My most liked colour is blue.
My (colour) preference differs for where I use it.
They makes me feel comfortable and relaxing.
Whenever I see the blue sky, I will forget my worries.
Blue is definitely my type. It's allow people to think of the ocean.
I wanna give a clean-cut but mellow image to the people I meet.
It enhances my personality which can create great image in society.
I always feel calm down when seeing this colour.

Can you see what's wrong with the words I've highlighted in red? Read my corrected versions below, and compare the changes carefully.

My favourite colour is blue. / The colour I like best is blue.
My (colour) preference differs depending on where I use it.
They make me feel comfortable and relaxed.
Whenever I see the blue sky, I forget my worries. (this is a 'zero' conditional)
Blue is definitely my favourite because it makes me think of the ocean.
I want to come across as smart but relaxed.*
It enhances my personality and helps me to come across as confident.
I always feel calm when I see this colour.**

*The phrasal verb 'come across as' is a good one to use when you want to talk about how you appear to other people. For example:
In a job interview, it's important to come across as confident and professional.

In number 6 above, I also changed the words "clean-cut but mellow". This phrase is completely fine, but I think I would probably say something like "smart but relaxed" in a normal conversation.

**Calm down is a phrasal verb, whereas calm is an adjective.
