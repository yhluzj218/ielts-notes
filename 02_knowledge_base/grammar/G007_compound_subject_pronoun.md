# G007 — Compound Subject: `My sister and I` (Not `me`)

## Core Rule

In a **compound subject** (two people doing something together), use the **subject pronoun** `I`, not the object pronoun `me`.

> `I` = subject (does the action)
> `me` = object (receives the action)

**Test:** Remove the other person. Would you say `Me am going` or `I am going`? → The answer tells you which form to use.

## Why it matters in IELTS

In Speaking Part 2, candidates often talk about experiences with family or friends. `"My sister and me went to..."` is a common error that signals basic grammar weakness.

## Common mistakes

❌ `My sister and me are going to the market.`
❌ `My friend and me were separated.`
❌ `Between you and I` → (object after preposition → should be `me`)

## Good examples

✅ `My sister and I are going to the market.`
✅ `My friend and I were separated at the airport.`
✅ `She and I have known each other for years.`

When `me` IS correct (object position):
✅ `He called my sister and me.` (object of the verb)
✅ `Between you and me, I think he's wrong.` (after preposition)

## Reusable patterns

**For Part 2 speaking:**
```
[Name] and I + [verb]: "My brother and I went to..."
[Name] and I + [be]: "My friend and I were both surprised."
```

## Mini practice

Choose the correct pronoun:

1. `My parents and (I/me) travelled to Japan last summer.`
2. `The teacher told my classmate and (I/me) to stay behind.`
3. `Between you and (I/me), I think the plan will fail.`

**Answers:**
1. **I** (subject)
2. **me** (object of `told`)
3. **me** (after preposition `between`)
