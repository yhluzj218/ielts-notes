# IELTS Advice: Master the method first

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

When students show me their essays, the first thing that I look for is evidence that they are following my writing method.
For example, I'm looking for a two-sentence introduction, main body paragraphs that contain five sentences each, and a one-sentence conclusion.
Unfortunately, some students go back to their old ways when they write essays. They write long introductions, unbalanced main paragraphs, conclusions that contain new information etc. etc.
Why does this happen?
I think the answer is that many people are impatient. They don't want to spend time mastering a writing method or planning an essay carefully.
Please don't be one of these students! Master the method first, so that you write every essay in exactly the same way. Then you can gradually improve other aspects of writing, like idea development, coherence, vocabulary range, and grammatical accuracy.
