# Model Essay: Parenting Course

日期：2026-07-07
類型：Task 2 — Agree/Disagree（To what extent）
作者查證：**Simon**（ielts-simon.com，英國籍、前雅思考官）。開頭段逐字比對 Wayback Machine 存檔頁面確認為 Simon 本人具名發表：[archive.org 存檔（2018-07-18 原文）](https://web.archive.org/web/20180722074531/http://ielts-simon.com/ielts-help-and-english-pr/2018/07/ielts-writing-task-2-introduction-and-conclusion.html)

## 題目

Caring for children is probably the most important job in any society. Because of this, all mothers and fathers should be required to take a course that prepares them to be good parents. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this view?

## 範本全文

It is true that parents shoulder a huge responsibility and that raising children is by no means an easy task. However, I completely disagree with the idea that we should therefore force all mothers and fathers to attend parenting courses.

In my opinion, the idea that all future parents should take a parenthood preparation course is completely impractical. Many prospective parents have jobs and busy schedules, and they may not be willing or able to attend regular parenting classes. This raises the question of whether those who missed the classes, or perhaps refused to attend, would be punished. I believe that it would be wrong to do this, and it would therefore be impossible to enforce the idea of compulsory training for parents. Besides, even if parents could be forced to attend, I doubt that people would agree on what good parenting entails, and so it would be difficult to create a parenting course to suit everyone.

As well as being impractical, I would argue that training courses for parents are unnecessary. Mothers and fathers have been raising children without any formal help or official interference for thousands of years. Parenting skills are learnt from family members, friends, neighbours and the surrounding culture. Perhaps more importantly, adults learn to be good parents by instinct, by trial and error, and by getting to know their own children; for example, a good parent will try different strategies when faced with a badly-behaved child, and will gradually develop an understanding of what works to correct the behaviour. None of this requires the intervention of a taught course.

In conclusion, while compulsory parenting lessons might seem like a good idea, I believe that such a scheme would be unworkable and largely pointless.

### 預估 Band：8–9

- **Task Response：** 立場從第一句就明確（讓步「責任重大」+ 立刻反駁「但不代表要強制上課」），完全對應題目的 "because of this" 前提——這正是你的筆記 `writing_notes.md` 強調的「to what extent」題型必須直接回應前提，不能只講自己的理由。兩個 body 給了兩個獨立、清楚區分的理由（不可行 / 不必要），每個理由都有解釋+具體例子，沒有停在空泛敘述。
- **Coherence & Cohesion：** 沒有用 firstly/secondly/thirdly 這種機械式列點（呼應 Joe 的警告：linking words ≠ 連貫），段落用 "Besides"、"As well as being impractical, I would argue that..." 這種自然承接句連起來，讀起來像真的在推進論證，不是條列清單。
- **Lexical Resource：** 用詞自然但不刻意堆砌高階字（"shoulder a huge responsibility"、"by no means an easy task"、"trial and error" 都是自然搭配，不是硬塞的單字），符合你「不缺詞彙，不用往難字方向想」的原則。
- **Grammar：** 句型有變化（讓步句、假設語氣「even if parents could be forced」、分詞構句），沒有明顯錯誤。

### 這篇好在哪（我要學的結構/手法）

- **開頭讓步 + 反駁**：先承認題目前提合理（"parents shoulder a huge responsibility"），再清楚反駁題目的結論（強制上課）——這正是你老師教的「to what extent」題型必須做的事，不能跳過前提直接講自己的理由。
- **兩個 body 分工清楚、不重疊**：Body 1 打「可行性」（enforcement 問題、標準不一），Body 2 打「必要性」（本來就能透過家庭/文化/試錯學會）——兩個角度完全不同，不是同一個論點換句話說。
- **每個論點都收在具體例子/結果**：Body 2 沒有停在「憑本能學習」這種抽象敘述，而是給了一個具體情境（"a good parent will try different strategies when faced with a badly-behaved child"）才收尾，符合你的 `writing_patterns.md` §6 Body Paragraph Chain 規則。
- **結論只重申立場，沒有加新論點**：最後一句用「while 讓步 + 我的立場」收尾，跟開頭首尾呼應。

### 值得學的表達（自然、可借用）

⚠️ 這些都是「自然會用到」等級，不是堆高級字：

- `shoulder a huge responsibility` — 表達「承擔重大責任」，比 "have a big responsibility" 更自然、更書面。適用情境：任何討論責任歸屬的題目（父母、政府、企業）。
- `by no means an easy task` — 承認某件事很困難的自然說法，用在讓步句開頭很順。
- `trial and error` — 「試錯」的自然說法，講學習過程、技能養成時很好用（不限親職主題）。
- `unworkable and largely pointless` — 收尾時評論一個提案「行不通又沒意義」的自然說法，比單用 "bad idea" 更精準。

### 連接詞 / 句型片語（可直接套用）

- **開頭讓步+反駁**：`It is true that [X]. However, I completely disagree with the idea that [Y].`
  用在：Agree/Disagree 題型的 intro，尤其題目有 "because of this" 這種前提句時。
- **段落承接（不是 firstly/secondly）**：`As well as being [第一個論點的形容詞], I would argue that [第二個論點].`
  用在：Body 2 開頭，自然銜接 Body 1，不需要制式連接詞。
- **舉例句型**：`..., for example, [具體情境], and will gradually [結果/發展].`
  用在：段落收尾前的具體例子，例子後面要接一個結果，不能例子完就結束（呼應 Never end a paragraph with an example 規則）。
- **結論讓步句**：`In conclusion, while [對方論點]  might seem like a good idea, I believe that [你的立場].`
  用在：Conclusion，跟 intro 的讓步+反駁首尾呼應。

### Reusable Ideas / Mental Model

以下兩個論點通過「半年後還能用在別題」的標準：

**1. 「強制規定」在執行面通常站不住腳（enforcement 問題）**
- Claim → 提議把某件事變成強制性規定，在現實中通常難以執行
- Reason → 「強制」需要明確的標準和懲罰機制，但很多議題本來就沒有公認的統一標準
- Mechanism → 誰該被排除、誰該被處罰、標準怎麼訂，這些問題本身就很難有共識，導致規定變得難以落實或顯得不公平
- Impact → 提案聽起來立意良善，但實際上不可行，最後淪為空談
- Example → 強制所有父母上親職課程：誰沒去要不要處罰？「好父母」的標準本來就沒有共識，課程要怎麼設計才適合所有人？
→ 已存到 `10_knowledge_base/ideas/government-society/mandatory-proposals-often-fail-on-enforcement.md`

**2. 技能可以透過非正式管道（經驗、試錯、環境）習得，不一定需要正式訓練**
- Claim → 很多能力是靠實作、試錯、耳濡目染學會的，不代表一定要靠正式課程/訓練才能習得
- Reason → 人類長期以來本來就是透過這些非正式方式獲得這類能力，正式課程不是唯一或必要途徑
- Mechanism → 透過真實情境中不斷嘗試、調整、觀察他人，逐漸建立起用得上的判斷力，這種學習方式反而更貼近實際情境
- Impact → 「應該開設正式課程來教這個」這類提案的必要性因此被削弱
- Example → 親職技巧：長期以來父母都是從家人、朋友、周遭文化，以及自己跟孩子相處的試錯過程中學會的，不需要靠正式課程
→ 已存到 `10_knowledge_base/ideas/education/skills-can-be-learned-informally-without-formal-training.md`
