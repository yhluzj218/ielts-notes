## Section 2 — Vocabulary / Chunk Input (5 min)

Today's chunks — Part 3 opinion language (societal level):

1. play a significant role in
   — Meaning: to have an important effect on something; used when something matters a lot
   — Example: "Education plays a significant role in reducing inequality."

2. have a profound impact on
   — Meaning: stronger than "affect" — deep, serious, lasting effect
   — Example: "Social media has had a profound impact on the way people communicate."

3. tend to
   — Meaning: used to describe a general pattern or what usually happens
   — Example: "Younger generations tend to prefer digital communication over face-to-face interaction."

4. in the long run
   — Meaning: over a long period of time; looking at the final outcome, not the immediate result
   — Example: "In the long run, investing in education is more effective than short-term spending."

5. as a result of
   — Meaning: because of; used to show a cause-and-effect connection
   — Example: "As a result of rapid urbanisation, many cities are facing housing shortages."

## Section 3 — Listening Focus (5 min)

Today's skill: Reversal signals

Reversal signals are words that flip the direction of what a speaker says.
The speaker starts with one idea — then reverses or corrects it.
The correct answer comes AFTER the reversal.

Key reversal words: but · however · actually · not anymore · used to · originally · instead · although · on the other hand

Example 1:
"The museum was open on Sundays... but not anymore. It's now closed on weekends."
→ Do not write "open." The answer is "closed."

Example 2:
"Originally the course cost $200... however, the price has been reduced to $150."
→ The answer is $150, not $200.

Example 3:
"She used to work in the city centre, but she's actually based in the northern branch now."
→ The answer is "northern branch," not "city centre."

Tomorrow's S4 practice — listen for reversal signals and underline them in the transcript during your analysis.

## Section 4 — Speaking Drill (10 min)

Part 3 question:

"Do you think governments should invest more money in public transportation? Why or why not?"

--- THINKING TIME: 60 seconds. Pause here. Think of: your opinion → one reason → one societal example → long-term effect. ---

Now speak your answer. Aim for 4–5 sentences.

[SILENCE — speak for about 45–60 seconds before continuing]

Sample answer:
"Yes, I think governments should definitely prioritise public transportation investment. When more people use buses and trains instead of private cars, it reduces traffic congestion and air pollution significantly — and that has a profound impact on public health, especially in densely populated cities. There's also an economic argument: in the long run, maintaining public transport networks tends to be far more cost-effective than building more roads for private vehicles. I think this is particularly true in countries that are still expanding their urban areas — investing now means fewer problems later."

Notice: opinion stated first → reason → societal example → long-term effect. No personal story needed for Part 3.
