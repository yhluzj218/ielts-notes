## Sentence Pattern

There are, however, drawbacks associated with [development or situation].

### Function

Introduce the disadvantages of a development while signalling a contrast with
benefits discussed earlier.

### Structure

`There are, however, drawbacks associated with + noun phrase.`

### Usage

Use this pattern as a formal topic sentence when moving from advantages to
disadvantages. It is especially useful in an advantages–disadvantages essay or a
balanced discussion.

The commas around `however` are required because it interrupts the clause. Follow
the sentence with specific drawbacks and their consequences.

### Example

There are, however, drawbacks associated with the widespread use of facial
recognition technology.

### IELTS Topics

- Technology
- Tourism
- Transport
- Education
- Work and employment
- Globalisation
