# L004 — Note Completion Pacing: Move On, Don't Chase

## Core Rule

**If you miss one answer, leave a dash (`—`) and immediately move to the next question.**

Do NOT spend time trying to recover the missed answer while the recording continues. You will lose TWO answers instead of one.

## Why it matters in IELTS

Note completion (especially in S4) runs at a continuous pace. Losing focus on one question for too long causes a cascade of errors. One blank is recoverable; three missed questions is not.

## Strategy

1. **Pre-read:** read ALL questions before the recording starts
2. **During:** write when you have the answer; dash when you don't
3. **When you miss one:** say "skip" in your head and look at the NEXT question immediately
4. **On review:** go back to the dash — sometimes context from later answers helps you recall

## Pacing rule for S4

S4 is a monologue (lecture, talk) — it moves faster than conversations. The questions follow the recording in order, so:
- If you're still on question 35 and the recording is clearly at question 37 → skip 35 and 36, catch 37
