# SP004 — Part 1 Strategy: Answer Directly, Then Stop

## Core Rule

Part 1 tests **direct, natural conversation**. Answer the question, then stop. The examiner will continue the conversation.

> No need to over-explain. No need to prove your vocabulary.

## Why it matters in IELTS

Part 1 is not the place for extended opinions — that's Part 3. Over-explaining in Part 1 wastes time and sounds unnatural. Examiners want to hear a range of questions, not one long answer.

## Length guideline

| Question type | Appropriate length |
|---|---|
| Choice question (`Do you prefer...?`) | 1–3 sentences |
| `Why` question | 2–4 sentences |
| `How often / How long` | 1–2 sentences |
| `Describe` question | 2–4 sentences |

## Structure for each answer

```
Direct answer → brief reason or detail → stop.
```

**Example:**
> Q: Do you prefer to study alone or with others?
> A: "I prefer studying alone. I find it easier to concentrate without distractions. It depends on the subject though — for group projects, obviously I work with others."

## What to avoid

- Long philosophical explanations for simple questions
- Starting every answer with "That's a very interesting question..."
- Repeating the question back before answering

## Recovering from an unexpected question

If the question surprises you:
1. Take a breath (normal pause — examiners expect it)
2. Answer honestly, even if simple: `"Honestly, I've never really thought about that, but I suppose..."`
3. Add a brief reason or example
