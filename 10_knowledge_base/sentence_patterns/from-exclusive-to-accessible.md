## Sentence Pattern

[X] used to be the preserve of [privileged group]. Now even [wider group] have
access to [X].

### Function

Show that something once restricted to a privileged group has become widely
accessible.

### Structure

`[Activity, resource, or opportunity] + used to be the preserve of + [privileged
group]. Now even + [wider group] + have access to + [it/them].`

### Usage

Use this two-sentence pattern to develop a positive historical comparison about
wider access. The first sentence establishes the former barrier; the second shows
how access has expanded.

Make sure the pronoun in the second sentence clearly refers to the activity,
resource, or opportunity introduced in the first.

### Example

International education used to be the preserve of wealthy families. Now even
students on modest incomes have access to overseas study programmes.

### IELTS Topics

- Education
- Technology
- Tourism
- Culture
- Health
- Social inequality
