## 題目

Some people think students should only study subjects that are useful, such as science and technology. Others believe students should study whatever they like. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

## 範文全文（~280 字）

Some subjects, such as science and technology, are widely regarded as "useful", and many people therefore believe that students should study only these rather than the subjects they truly enjoy. This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my opinion, arguing that students should study according to their preferences.

There is no doubt that a solid knowledge of fundamental subjects is essential to a student's later studies. Imagine that you have a strong grasp of mathematics: it genuinely helps you comprehend physics, because the concepts themselves are closely connected to many other fields of knowledge. This is the main reason why science and technology are seen as prerequisites for most other subjects. The more effort students put into these areas, the greater the competitive advantage they gain in the long term.

On the other hand, other people, including myself, believe that the dominant factor in keeping a person studying consistently is not how practical the subject is — it is eagerness and passion. A student who only studies subjects considered "essential", with no interest in them, is likely to burn out and give up. Allowing students to study whatever they like hands the decision back to them, and they tend to perform better when learning of their own will. My cousin, for example, struggled through compulsory chemistry but flourished once she switched to design, which she loved; each achievement pushed her to pursue a higher goal, resulting in a profound and long-lasting sense of accomplishment.

To sum up, although fundamental knowledge is important and worth studying, it should not be the only focus. I believe that letting pupils explore the subjects they love brings far more lasting value.

## 為什麼這樣改（從 Connie 原稿 Band ~6 → 7–7.5 的改動邏輯）

> 這個區塊是這篇範本的核心價值：範文不是「別人的好文章」，是「你自己的文章修到 7 的樣子」。

**1. GRA 5.5 → 7 的關鍵：修掉 8 處主謂一致（G007），零新句型**
- `people believes` → `many people believe` · `it actually help` → `it genuinely helps` · `students tends` → `they tend` · `The concept itself are` → `the concepts themselves are`
- 句子結構完全沒有升級——光是動詞跟對主詞，GRA 就跳一級。**這是全篇最便宜的分數。**

**2. 詞性修正（LR 靠精準，不靠難字）**
- `a competitive advantageous` → `competitive advantage`（adj → noun）
- `the eager and passion does` → `it is eagerness and passion`（adj → noun + 單複數）
- `under her/his willing` → `of their own will`（adj → noun）
- 整篇沒有一個 Connie 不認識的字。7 分 = 字用對，不是字更難（Matt + Joe 共同確認的方向）。

**3. 唯一新增的內容：Body 2 具體例子（TR 6.5 → 7）**
- 原稿 Body 2 chain 有 Point → Explain → Result，但 Example 空缺，整段都是概括的 "a student"。
- 加一個具名的人（my cousin, chemistry → design）讓 chain 落地——與 7/11 Diana 例子同一招。例子概括性即可，不需真實精確（Joe 7/9）。

**4. 銜接（CC）**
- Body 2 開頭 `However` → `On the other hand, other people, including myself, believe...`：However 帶反駁味，Discussion 換邊要中性對比；立場內嵌在段首後，原稿段尾指涉不明的 `That is why I agree with this statement` 就能直接刪掉。
- `The more efforts you spend..., it reward you...` → `The more effort..., the greater the competitive advantage...`：原稿想用的 the more... the more... 結構，把後半句接完就好。
- 結論 `I strongly agree` → `I believe`：agree/disagree 是另一個題型的用語，Discussion 結論不用它。

**5. 刪掉的：intro 前兩句 filler**
- `There are various subjects in school time.` 整句刪除，四句 intro 壓成兩句半（Simon：intro = 2 句）。資訊零損失，省下的字數給了 Body 2 的例子。

**為什麼是 7–7.5 而不是更高：** 論點深度與字彙難度刻意維持原稿水準。8 分需要更精細的立場推進和幾乎零錯——現在的目標是讓「已經會的東西」穩定產出 7。

### 預估 Band

**7–7.5。** TR 7.5：題型正確、雙方都認真論述、立場貫穿全文、Body 2 有具體例子收尾。CC 7：段落各司其職、換邊訊號清楚、the more...the greater 等結構自然推進。LR 7：用字精準自然、無堆砌（widely regarded as / prerequisites for / of their own will）。GRA 7：句型有複雜度（分號、讓步、比較結構）且錯誤極少。未到 8 是因為論點推進和修辭密度仍屬扎實而非出色——這是刻意的天花板。

### 這篇好在哪（我要學的結構/手法）

- **Body 1 公平論證弱方**：全段零反駁，reason（學科相連）→ example（數學→物理）→ consequence（長期優勢），Matt 6/26 教的原則的完整示範——而且這段的想法本來就是 Connie 自己的
- **換邊訊號**：`On the other hand, other people, including myself, believe...` 一句同時完成「轉段 + 表態」，Body 2 結尾不需要再宣告立場
- **Intro 兩句收工**：句 1 = 改寫兩觀點、句 2 = thesis（討論雙方 + 亮立場），沒有 filler
- **例子的寫法**：`My cousin, for example, struggled through X but flourished once she switched to Y` —— 一句話裡有 before/after 對比，自帶 Result

### 值得學的表達（自然、可借用）

- `be widely regarded as X` — 「被普遍視為」，intro 改寫題目立場時的中性寫法（比 people think 高半級但完全自然）
- `a strong grasp of [subject]` — 「對某科目掌握扎實」，教育題萬用；grasp 當名詞
- `prerequisites for [X]` — 「X 的先修條件」，教育／技能題（介系詞是 for）
- `burn out and give up` — 「倦怠而放棄」，動機／壓力類主題
- `of their own will` — 「出於自己的意願」，自主權類論點（不是 under one's willing）
- `a sense of accomplishment` — 「成就感」，個人發展題固定搭配（不是 wellness）
- `flourish` — 「蓬勃發展／如魚得水」，和 struggle 成對使用效果最好
