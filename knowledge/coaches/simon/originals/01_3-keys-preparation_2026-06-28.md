# The 3 Keys to IELTS Preparation

If you want to improve your IELTS score, I believe that you should do the following three things:
Have a method for each part of the test.
Improve your English as part of your preparation.
Do realistic test practice.
Just remember: method, English, practice. This sums up my approach in a very simple way, so let's look at each of these 'keys' in a bit more detail.

## 1. Method

I aim to give you a step-by-step method for every aspect of the listening, reading, speaking and writing tests.

A good method should tell you exactly what to do in every situation. Having a 'method for everything' is a great way to ensure that you feel ready and confident on your exam day.

## 2. English

Many students forget that IELTS is an English language test. They spend too much time on 'tips and techniques', hoping to find a shortcut to success. Others do some work on language, but they focus mainly on grammar. They try, for example, to learn 'complex structures'.

I believe that vocabulary is the key to a higher IELTS score. If you can widen the range of words and phrases that you are able to use correctly, you'll become a better user of English.

## 3. Practice

You might think that practice means doing an IELTS test at home to see what score you get. However, there are other ways to practise.

For example, you could spend some time practising just one type of reading question (e.g. true, false, not given). Or you could use a study session to work only on writing introductions.

I like to call this type of focused practice 'training'. Instead of just testing yourself, you can train yourself to become better at small parts of the test.
