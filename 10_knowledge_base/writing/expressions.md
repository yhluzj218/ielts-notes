# Reusable Expressions & Collocations

> Cross-topic language that isn't tied to one skeleton position — organised by
> **function**, not by where it sits in an essay. For position-based sentence frames
> (Introduction / Body / Conclusion), see [`sentence_patterns.md`](sentence_patterns.md)
> instead — check both before adding a new entry so nothing gets duplicated.
> Last updated: 2026-07-04

---

## What belongs here

Two kinds of entry, both must be genuinely reusable across different topics —
not just something that happened to work well in one essay:

1. **Functional Language** — short sentence frames that signal a function (cause,
   consequence, comparison, concession, scale) and can be dropped into any topic.
2. **Natural Collocations** — 2–4 word chunks that are high-frequency, natural, and
   not topic-specific vocabulary.

Every entry has a **Learning Status**: `New` (just added) → `Practised` (used at
least once in real Writing or Speaking) → `Internalised` (comes out naturally without
conscious recall). Update the status yourself when you notice it happening, or tell
me and I'll update it during `finish today` / `批改`. For an actual technique to move
an entry from `New` toward `Internalised` (image + gesture + spoken output, not rote
reading), see `10_knowledge_base/practice_method.md`.

Every entry also has a **Podcast Candidate** flag (`Yes` / `No`): does this sound
natural if a character *says it out loud* in a scene (ConnieVerse), not just writes
it in an essay? Formal/written-register phrasing gets `No` — it can still be great
for writing, it just isn't spoken-dialogue material. This is how
`07_podcast/daily_template.md` picks its Target Vocabulary automatically — see
`CLAUDE.md` § 幫我準備明天的 Podcast 行為.

Do not add:
- Idea-specific phrasing that only makes sense for one argument (that stays in the
  idea note's own `Useful Chunks` section, e.g. `10_knowledge_base/ideas/<theme>/*.md`)
- Whole sentences lifted from one essay verbatim without checking they generalise

| Expression | Takes | Learning Status | Podcast Candidate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| `Even though [conceded], [claim].` | clause (same sentence) | New | Yes | |

| `[Claim]. However, [contrast].` | new sentence | New | Yes | Never combine with `although` in the same sentence |

| `[Claim]. That said, [contrast].` | new sentence | New | Yes | |

## Update Rules

- Add an entry only if it would still sound natural in a completely different topic
- Check `sentence_patterns.md` first — if it's tied to a specific skeleton slot
  (Introduction / Body opener / Conclusion), it belongs there instead
- Never paste a full sentence lifted from one essay without confirming it generalises
- Update Learning Status as evidence comes in — don't just keep appending `New` entries
  without ever promoting old ones (see `10_knowledge_base/ARCHITECTURE.md` § Learning Status)
- Judge Podcast Candidate at the same time you add the entry — don't leave it blank
