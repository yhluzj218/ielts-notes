# W003 — Adjective vs Noun Before a Noun

## Core Rule

When modifying a noun, use an **adjective**, not another noun.

> `loyal customer` ✅ (adjective `loyal` + noun `customer`)
> `loyalty customer` ❌ (noun `loyalty` cannot modify another noun directly)

Similarly, **nationality adjectives** (not country names) modify nouns:
> `a Dutch and Japanese colony` ✅
> `a colony of Dutch and Japan` ❌ (`Japan` is a noun, not an adjective)

## Why it matters in IELTS

Choosing the wrong word form (noun where adjective is needed) is classified as a Lexical Resource error. It's common when the correct adjective form is less obvious.

## Common mistakes

❌ `loyalty customer` → should be `loyal customer`
❌ `a colony of Dutch and Japan` → should be `a Dutch and Japanese colony`
❌ `a beauty day` → should be `a beautiful day`
❌ `a health lifestyle` → should be `a healthy lifestyle`

## Good examples

✅ `a loyal customer` / `loyal customers`
✅ `a Dutch and Japanese colony`
✅ `a healthy lifestyle`
✅ `a significant improvement`

## Reusable pairs to remember

| Noun | Adjective | Example |
|---|---|---|
| loyalty | loyal | loyal customer |
| beauty | beautiful | beautiful scenery |
| health | healthy | healthy habits |
| success | successful | successful business |
| wealth | wealthy | wealthy nation |
| Japan | Japanese | Japanese culture |
| China | Chinese | Chinese tradition |
| education | educational | educational system |

## Mini practice

Use the correct adjective form:

1. `They want to attract `loyalty` customers.` → ?
2. `She leads a `health` lifestyle.` → ?
3. `It was a `Japan` product.` → ?

**Answers:**
1. **loyal** customers
2. **healthy** lifestyle
3. **Japanese** product
