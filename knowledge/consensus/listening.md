### MCQ — Differentiating Keywords (not all keywords)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Source | Simon: `listening-mcq-method_2026-06-28.md` |
| Principle | In MCQ preparation, underline only the words that create a difference in meaning *between* options — not every keyword |
| Why | Words shared across all options are not useful; only the words where options diverge will be paraphrased differently in the audio |
| During audio | Only look at underlined words — do not re-read full sentences |
| Status | ⚠️ Simon only |

### Three Listening Trick Types

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Source | Simon: `listening-tricks_2026-06-28.md` |
| Status | ⚠️ Simon only |

**Trick 1 — Multiple options, one eliminated by qualifier**

The audio mentions more than one answer. The question specifies a condition that determines which answer is correct. Do not write the first number/word heard — track which one matches the question's condition.

Example: "12 for students, but 8 for members of the public." Question asks about "members of the public" → 8.

**Trick 2 — Speaker self-correction**

The speaker gives an initial answer and then corrects it. The answer is always the correction, not the first answer.

Self-correction signals: "Oh no, sorry..." · "Actually, it's..." · "I mean..." · "Sorry, I made a mistake..."

**Trick 3 — Conditional branches**

Different answers are given for different people or situations. Read the question to identify which condition applies, then track only that branch in the audio.

**Universal rule for all three tricks:** Do not write the first answer heard. Keep listening until you confirm the answer that satisfies the exact condition in the question.

**Direct connection to Connie:** All three tricks exploit "Premature Conclusion Before Verification" — the correct answer always comes after a qualifier, correction, or condition. The trap is always the first plausible word/number.
