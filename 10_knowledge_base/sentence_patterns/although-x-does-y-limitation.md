## Sentence Pattern

Although [X] does [provide a benefit], [limitation].

### Function

Acknowledge that a benefit is real before explaining why it is limited or less
valuable than it initially appears.

### Structure

`Although + subject + do/does + base verb + benefit, + limitation.`

### Usage

Use this concession pattern when you do not want to deny the opposing point
completely. The auxiliary `do` or `does` adds emphasis and confirms that the benefit
is genuine.

Do not add `however` to the same sentence. Make the subject of the second clause
explicit if a pronoun could refer to more than one noun.

### Example

Although online education does improve access, many students struggle without
face-to-face support.
