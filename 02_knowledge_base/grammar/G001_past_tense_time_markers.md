# G001 — Past Time Marker Forces Simple Past

## Core Rule

When a sentence contains an **explicit past time marker**, the verb must be **simple past** — never present perfect.

> Past time marker = any word that points to a **finished**, **specific** point in time.

Common past time markers: `last week`, `last year`, `yesterday`, `in 2010`, `ago`, `when I was`, `at that time`

**Rule:** Present perfect (`have/has + past participle`) describes experiences or states connected to **now**. Once you name a specific past moment, that connection to now is gone → simple past only.

## Why it matters in IELTS

Both Writing and Speaking examiners assess **Grammatical Range and Accuracy**. Tense errors are visible immediately. This error is a direct transfer from Chinese (我上週「有」買) and appears in both skills.

## Common mistakes

❌ `Last week I have purchased a new phone.`
❌ `Yesterday she has finished her assignment.`
❌ `In 2010, we have moved to a new city.`
❌ `When I was a child, I have always wanted to be a doctor.`

## Good examples

✅ `Last week I purchased a new phone.`
✅ `Yesterday she finished her assignment.`
✅ `In 2010, we moved to a new city.`
✅ `When I was a child, I always wanted to be a doctor.`

Present perfect without a time marker (correct):
✅ `I have purchased a new phone recently.` (recent, no fixed time point)
✅ `She has finished her assignment.` (completed, relevant now)

## Reusable patterns

| Time marker present? | Correct tense |
|---|---|
| `last week / yesterday / in [year] / ago` | Simple past: `I bought / she went` |
| No time marker, or `recently / before / ever` | Present perfect: `I have bought / she has gone` |

**Quick self-check:** Before using `have/has`, ask — "Does this sentence name a specific past time?" If yes → change to simple past.

## Mini practice

Fix the tense in each sentence:

1. `Last month, the company has launched a new product.`
2. `When she was 10, she has started learning piano.`
3. `I have visited Paris in 2018.`

**Answers:**
1. Last month, the company **launched** a new product.
2. When she was 10, she **started** learning piano.
3. I **visited** Paris in 2018.
