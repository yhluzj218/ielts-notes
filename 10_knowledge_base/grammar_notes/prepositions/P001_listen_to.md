# P001 — `listen to` (Preposition Required)

## Core Rule

`listen` is an **intransitive verb** — it cannot take a direct object without a preposition.
The correct form is always: **`listen to` + object**

> Contrast with `hear`, which IS transitive: `I heard the music.` (no preposition needed)

## Why it matters in IELTS

In Speaking, describing daily habits and activities is common (Part 1 and Part 2). Omitting `to` after `listen` is an immediate and visible error.

## Common mistakes

❌ `I enjoy listening that story.`
❌ `She was listening music in her room.`
❌ `Let's listen the radio.`

## Good examples

✅ `I enjoy listening to that story.`
✅ `She was listening to music in her room.`
✅ `Let's listen to the radio.`
✅ `I love listening to podcasts during my commute.`

**`hear` vs `listen to`:**
- `hear` = passive/accidental: `I heard a strange noise.`
- `listen to` = active/intentional: `I was listening to the news.`

## Reusable patterns

```
listen to + [music / podcasts / the radio / a speech / advice / someone]
I listen to [X] every day / when I commute / to relax.
```

## Mini practice

Add the missing preposition where needed:

1. `He spends hours listening _____ jazz at night.`
2. `Did you hear _____ that noise just now?`
3. `I always listen _____ English podcasts during my commute.`

**Answers:**
1. listening **to** jazz
2. `hear` = no preposition needed
3. listen **to** English podcasts
