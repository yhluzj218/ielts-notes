# Output Drill Script — International tourism

Context used:
07_podcast/source_briefs/2026-07-05_notebooklm_source_brief_international-tourism.md

Core Pattern:
Although [X] does [provide a benefit], [limitation].

## ElevenLabs-ready Script

Welcome back! Today, we are turning your ideas about international tourism into
spoken English.

You have already explored how tourism can bring benefits while also creating
pressure for local communities and the environment. Now let us practise one
balanced pattern, five useful expressions, and two new IELTS topics.

Let us get started!

[pause 3 seconds]

Listen to today's sentence first:

Although international tourism does bring benefits, it may still create problems
for local communities.

[pause 3 seconds]

This sentence does two things.

First, it accepts that international tourism has real benefits.

Then, it adds an important problem for local communities.

That is why the sentence sounds balanced. You are not saying tourism is completely
good or completely bad. You are showing both sides in one sentence.

Listen to the contrast:

International tourism does bring benefits.

[pause 3 seconds]

But it may still create problems for local communities.

[pause 3 seconds]

Put the two ideas together:

Although international tourism does bring benefits, it may still create problems
for local communities.

[pause 6 seconds]

One small language point: after "does," say "bring." So the phrase is "does bring
benefits."

Use this sentence shape when you want to accept one good point and then explain why
there is still a concern.

Now let us focus on your five expressions.

The first expression is "cultural awareness."

Cultural awareness means understanding and recognising cultures, customs, and
perspectives that may be different from your own.

Example:

International travel can increase cultural awareness when visitors engage
respectfully with local traditions.

Now repeat:

Cultural awareness.

[pause 3 seconds]

The second expression is "local communities."

Local communities are the people who live in the place affected by a development
or policy.

Example:

Tourism policies should consider the needs of local communities as well as the
experience of visitors.

Now repeat:

Local communities.

[pause 3 seconds]

The third expression is "seasonal work."

Seasonal work is employment that is available mainly during a particular part of
the year.

Example:

In a destination with a short peak season, some tourism jobs may depend on seasonal
work.

Now repeat:

Seasonal work.

[pause 3 seconds]

The fourth expression is "unevenly distributed."

If something is unevenly distributed, different people or places receive very
different shares of it.

Example:

Tourism income may be unevenly distributed between outside companies and local
businesses.

Now repeat:

Unevenly distributed.

[pause 3 seconds]

The fifth expression is "environmental pressure."

Environmental pressure means stress or harm placed on natural resources,
ecosystems, or the physical environment.

Example:

High visitor numbers can create environmental pressure in popular destinations.

Now repeat:

Environmental pressure.

[pause 3 seconds]

Now it is time for shadowing.

Listen to the complete model sentence first.

Although international tourism does bring benefits, it may still create problems
for local communities.

[pause 3 seconds]

Now repeat the first natural chunk:

Although international tourism does bring benefits,

[pause 3 seconds]

Now repeat the limitation:

it may still create problems for local communities.

[pause 3 seconds]

Now say the complete sentence:

Although international tourism does bring benefits, it may still create problems
for local communities.

[pause 6 seconds]

One more time:

Although international tourism does bring benefits, it may still create problems
for local communities.

[pause 6 seconds]

Now let us move to interactive output.

[pause 3 seconds]
