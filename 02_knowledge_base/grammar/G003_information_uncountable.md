# G003 — `information` Is Always Uncountable

## Core Rule

`information` has **no plural form**. It cannot take `a` or `-s`.

To express "one piece," use: `a piece of information`

## Why it matters in IELTS

`informations` is immediately visible and marks a basic error. This is a transfer from Chinese (一個資訊 / 資訊們 doesn't apply to English).

## Common mistakes

❌ `I found some useful informations online.`
❌ `Can you give me an information?`
❌ `The informations are not accurate.`

## Good examples

✅ `I found some useful information online.`
✅ `Can you give me some information?`
✅ `Can you give me a piece of information?`
✅ `The information is not accurate.` (note: singular verb)

## Reusable patterns

| Intended meaning | Correct form |
|---|---|
| some info | `some information` |
| one fact | `a piece of information` |
| two facts | `two pieces of information` |
| all the info | `all the information` |

**Note:** `information` always takes a **singular verb**: `The information is...` not `are`.

## Mini practice

Correct the errors:

1. `The website provides many informations about the city.`
2. `She gave me an important information.`
3. `These informations will be useful for your research.`

**Answers:**
1. The website provides a lot of **information** about the city.
2. She gave me an important **piece of information**.
3. This **information** will be useful for your research.
