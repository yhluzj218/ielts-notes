## Expression

be severely damaged

### Function

Describe serious harm to something, especially when the focus is on the result
rather than who or what caused it.

### Usage

Use this passive expression for substantial physical, environmental, economic, or
reputational harm. `Severely` is a natural intensifier with `damaged` and signals
that the harm is serious.

For culture, prefer a more precise combination: `cultural heritage can be severely
damaged` or `local culture can be eroded`. The broad phrase `culture is damaged` is
understandable but less natural.

### Common Collocations

- be severely damaged by pollution
- be severely damaged by mass tourism
- be severely damaged during a natural disaster
- severely damaged infrastructure
- severely damaged reputation

### Example

Fragile ecosystems can be severely damaged by uncontrolled tourism.
