# G009 — Past Passive: `was/were + Past Participle`

## Core Rule

Passive voice in past tense requires: **`was` / `were` + past participle**

Both elements are required. The auxiliary (`was`/`were`) cannot be omitted.

> Active: `They separated us.`
> Passive: `We were separated.` ← must have `were`

## Why it matters in IELTS

Passive voice is used frequently in Task 2 academic writing to describe trends, events, and processes. Missing the auxiliary is a visible structural error.

## Common mistakes

❌ `We been separated.`
❌ `The building constructed in 1990.`
❌ `Many people affected by the crisis.`

## Good examples

✅ `We were separated at the airport.`
✅ `The building was constructed in 1990.`
✅ `Many people were affected by the crisis.`

**Present passive (for comparison):**
✅ `The building is maintained by the local council.` (am/is/are + past participle)

**Future passive:**
✅ `The policy will be implemented next year.` (will be + past participle)

## Reusable patterns

```
Past passive: [Subject] + was/were + [past participle] (+ by [agent])
The report was submitted on time.
Workers were paid a fair wage.
The law was changed in 2015.
```

**Irregular past participles to remember:**
- build → built
- write → written
- give → given
- make → made
- see → seen

## Mini practice

Complete with the correct passive form:

1. `The school _____ (build) 50 years ago.`
2. `Several students _____ (affect) by the new policy.`
3. `The letter _____ (send) before the deadline.`

**Answers:**
1. **was built**
2. **were affected**
3. **was sent**
