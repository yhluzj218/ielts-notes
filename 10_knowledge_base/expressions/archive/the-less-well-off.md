## Expression

the less well-off

### Function

Refer collectively to people with relatively little money in a neutral and
considerate way.

### Usage

Use this expression when discussing economic inequality or differences in access.
It is less blunt than `the poor` and works in both IELTS Writing and Speaking.

`Well-off` refers to financial circumstances. Do not use it for people who are
disadvantaged for unrelated reasons.

### Common Collocations

- support the less well-off
- opportunities for the less well-off
- make services accessible to the less well-off
- exclude the less well-off

### Example

Public transport subsidies can make employment opportunities more accessible to
the less well-off.

### IELTS Topics

- Social inequality
- Government spending
- Education
