# Reusable Ideas（跨題目論點庫）

> 收「想法」本身，不是語言。一個 idea 要能套進至少 3 個不同主題的作文，才值得收進來。
> 判斷標準：半年後看到，仍然可以直接拿來想另一篇作文的論點。
> 不是要背這些想法本身的文字，是要知道「有這個角度可以用」。
> Last updated: 2026-07-02

---

## Democratisation of Access（曾經專屬少數人的東西，現在變得普及）

Description: 某個活動/資源以前只有少數（通常是富裕）階層能享有，因為成本下降或技術進步，現在普通人也能取得。
Why Reusable: 這是「今昔對比」型論點，套用在任何「以前 vs 現在」的題目都能用，是正面（優點）角度的萬用開場論證。
Possible Topics:
- Technology（網路、手機普及）
- Education（線上課程、留學）
- Travel / Tourism
- Consumer goods（品牌商品、精品）
- Healthcare（醫療科技普及化）
Development: `[Point] X used to be the preserve of [privileged group] → [Explain] due to [cost drop / tech advance] → [Example] now [broader group] can access it → [Result] this has increased [equality/awareness/opportunity]`
Source: International Tourism model essay, 2026-07-02

## Commodification of Culture（文化因為商業化而失真）

Description: 當某個文化/傳統因為大量商業需求而被簡化、量產、包裝成「消費品」，失去原本的真實性。
Why Reusable: 適用於任何「傳統 vs 商業化/全球化」的衝突題目，是負面（缺點）角度的核心論點。
Possible Topics:
- Tourism（觀光化的傳統文化）
- Globalisation
- Media / Entertainment（節慶、音樂商業化）
- Arts and Culture
- Consumerism
Development: `[Point] increased demand from [outside group] leads to [traditional practice] being commercialised → [Explain] authenticity is sacrificed for mass appeal/profit → [Example] traditional crafts replaced by cheap mass-produced versions → [Result] cultural erosion / loss of authenticity`
Source: International Tourism model essay, 2026-07-02

## Short-Term Stake, Irresponsible Behaviour（沒有長期利害關係就不負責任）

Description: 當一個人只是短暫參與某個地方/系統，不會承擔長期後果，就比較不會在意自己的行為造成的影響。
Why Reusable: 這是解釋「為什麼某群人行為不負責任」的通用因果邏輯，不限於觀光。
Possible Topics:
- Tourism（遊客亂丟垃圾）
- Environment（企業短期獲利 vs 長期環境成本）
- Renting vs owning property
- Gig economy / short-term employment
- Corporate short-termism（追求季度獲利）
Development: `[Point] because [group] only stay/participate for a short time → [Explain] they don't experience the long-term consequences of their actions → [Example] tourists litter or behave badly since they won't live with the result → [Result] the cost is borne by others (residents/environment/future)`
Source: International Tourism model essay, 2026-07-02

## Job Creation ≠ Job Quality（創造就業機會不等於創造好工作）

Description: 一個產業/政策可能真的創造了就業機會，但工作本身可能不穩定、季節性、低薪，數量上的成長不代表品質好。
Why Reusable: 這是反駁「這帶來了就業機會，所以是好事」這種簡化論證的標準角度，用途極廣。
Possible Topics:
- Tourism / Hospitality industry
- Automation / Technology and employment
- Globalisation / outsourcing
- Gig economy
- Economic development in poorer countries
Development: `[Point] although [industry] does create employment → [Explain] the jobs tend to be precarious/seasonal/low-paid → [Example] long periods of the year with no work, low wages for frontline workers → [Result] the benefit of "job creation" is weaker than it first appears`
Source: International Tourism model essay, 2026-07-02

## Profit Leakage（利潤外流，當地經濟沒有真正受惠）

Description: 表面上一個地方因為某活動賺到錢，但實際上大部分利潤被外部的大企業/跨國公司拿走，沒有留在當地經濟裡。
Why Reusable: 這是反駁「這個活動讓當地變富有」這種說法的關鍵反例，常搭配 Job Creation ≠ Job Quality 一起用。
Possible Topics:
- Tourism
- Globalisation / foreign investment
- Big business vs local/small business
- Economic development in developing countries
- Outsourcing / multinational corporations
Development: `[Point] money spent by [group] is seldom retained by the local economy → [Explain] profits are taken out by [outside entity, e.g. multinational corporations] → [Example] a small number of big businesses profit while most workers earn low wages → [Result] the local community sees little real economic benefit`
Source: International Tourism model essay, 2026-07-02

## Individually Harmless, Collectively Damaging（個別看似無害，累積起來造成傷害）

Description: 單一個人的行為（例如一次旅行、一次消費）看起來影響很小，但當很多人一起做，累積起來的環境/社會影響就很大。
Why Reusable: 這是環境類題目的核心論證邏輯，幾乎所有「大量消費/大量行為」造成環境傷害的題目都能用這個角度。
Possible Topics:
- Environment / climate change
- Transport（開車、搭飛機）
- Consumerism（快時尚、一次性用品）
- Food（肉類消費）
- Tourism
Development: `[Point] [activity] adds to [environmental cost, e.g. greenhouse gases] → [Explain] each instance seems small, but the scale of [group] doing it is what causes the damage → [Example] international travel's cumulative emissions → [Result] leads to harmful climate change`
Source: International Tourism model essay, 2026-07-02
