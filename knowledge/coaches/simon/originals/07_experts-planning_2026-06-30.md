# Experts spend longer planning

> Source: Simon (IELTS Simon)
> Saved: 2026-06-30

Today I want to share an old lesson that I wrote about planning. The idea that experts spend longer planning can help you to become a better essay writer.
I read about some interesting research which compared physics professors and PhD students (experts) with physics undergraduates (not experts). The professors, PhD students and undergraduates were all given the same physics test.
Here's a summary of the research findings:
"Professors and PhDs were slower than undergraduates to begin to solve the problems. The experts paused before they put pencil to paper. They spent a few minutes assessing the structure of the problem and figuring out the best physics principle to use. The undergraduates, on the other hand, jumped right into problem solving, which often got them in trouble."
To summarise, the experts took some time to think and plan before tackling a problem, whereas the less experienced students jumped straight in.
Can you see how this might relate to IELTS preparation?
For example, most IELTS candidates jump straight in when it comes to the writing test. They start writing without taking time to assess, think and plan. And, as the study said, this often gets them in trouble!
