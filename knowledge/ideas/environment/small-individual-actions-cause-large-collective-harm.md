# Idea: Small individual actions cause large collective harm

## Theme
environment

## Core Idea
A single person's action (one trip, one purchase) looks negligible on its own, but
when huge numbers of people do the same thing, the cumulative environmental or social
impact becomes serious.

## Why
Environmental cost is often driven by scale, not by any single instance. Individually
harmless behaviour becomes damaging purely because of how many people repeat it — the
harm is in the aggregate, not the individual act.

## Example
International air travel's cumulative greenhouse gas emissions — one flight seems
minor, but millions of flights add up to a major contributor to climate change.

## Reusable IELTS Angle
Core causal logic for almost any environment question about mass consumption or mass
behaviour causing environmental damage.

## Writing Use
Body paragraph chain:
`[Point] [activity] adds to [environmental cost, e.g. greenhouse gases] → [Explain] each instance seems small, but the scale of [group] doing it is what causes the damage → [Example] international travel's cumulative emissions → [Result] leads to harmful climate change`

## Speaking Use
Part 3 answers to "Do individual actions really make a difference to the
environment?": "On their own, not much — but the problem is that when millions of
people do the same thing, like flying or buying fast fashion, it adds up to a huge
impact."

## Useful Chunks
- "each instance seems small"
- "the scale of [group] doing it is what causes the damage"
- "adds up to a huge/cumulative impact"
