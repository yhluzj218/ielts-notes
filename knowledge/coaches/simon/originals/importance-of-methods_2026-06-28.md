# The Importance of Methods

When people ask me for advice about how to improve their IELTS scores, the first thing that I say to them is: you need a method for everything.

Do you have a method or a way of approaching every part of the test? If you don't, you're not as well prepared as you could be.

Two study tips:
Make a list of the IELTS exam methods that you already use.
Add to this list as you study the lessons on this site and learn more methods.

Some ideas to get you started:
In speaking part 1, we answer each question directly then give a reason. One sentence is enough.
In academic writing task 1, we write the introduction by paraphrasing the question statement.
In the reading test, it's best to do 'paragraph headings' questions last.

Keep your methods list with you as you study and practice. I'm sure this will help you.

## The method isn't working!

When IELTS students are stuck on the same score, they sometimes think that the methods they have learnt aren't working. But this is the wrong way to think.

Methods or exam techniques will only help you to get the score that reflects your current level of English. If your English level is beginner or intermediate, no method will suddenly give you a band 7.

Exam techniques don't increase your vocabulary knowledge or your grammatical accuracy.

Remember that there are 3 keys to IELTS preparation:
Exam methods
Improving your English
Improving by practising

If you've been following my advice but you're stuck on the same score, don't change your methods! Instead, look at points 2 and 3. Work on your English, and keep practising.

## Fluctuating scores

Some students complain that their scores fluctuate (go up and down) every time they take the test. For example:

"I got a band 6.5 in writing three months ago, and this time I only got 5.5. Why has my score gone down? I thought I was improving!"

There are two reasons why scores may fluctuate like this:
The candidate didn't use a writing method.
The candidate tried a different writing method in each test.

To avoid score fluctuations, you need to write in a consistent way.

Remember: You can only track and measure your progress if you use the same methods or exam techniques every time you take the test.

To make steady progress and avoid ups and downs, be more methodical.
