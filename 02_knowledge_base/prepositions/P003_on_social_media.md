# P003 — `on social media` (Not `in social medium`)

## Core Rule

Two errors combined in the common mistake `in social medium`:

1. **Wrong preposition:** platforms/media use `on`, not `in`
2. **Wrong form:** the plural is `social media` — `medium` is the singular (almost never used in everyday speech)

**Always:** `on social media`

## Why it matters in IELTS

Social media topics appear frequently in Task 2. The phrase `on social media` is essential vocabulary. Using `in social medium` signals a fundamental error.

## Common mistakes

❌ `People share their opinions in social medium.`
❌ `She advertises her business in social media.`
❌ `We can spread information through social mediums.`

## Good examples

✅ `People share their opinions on social media.`
✅ `She advertises her business on social media.`
✅ `Information spreads rapidly on social media.`

**Preposition pattern — `on` for platforms:**
✅ `on Facebook / Instagram / Twitter / YouTube`
✅ `on the internet` (but `in an email` / `in a message`)
✅ `on television / on the radio` (but `in a newspaper / in a magazine`)

## Reusable patterns

```
on social media
on [platform name]
through social media channels
via social media
```

**Useful collocations with social media:**
- `spread information on social media`
- `go viral on social media`
- `advertise on social media`
- `connect with others through social media`

## Mini practice

Fix the preposition or noun form:

1. `Young people spend hours in social medium every day.`
2. `The news spread quickly in social media.`
3. `She built her brand through social mediums.`

**Answers:**
1. Young people spend hours **on social media** every day.
2. The news spread quickly **on social media**.
3. She built her brand through social media **channels** (or simply: **on social media**).
