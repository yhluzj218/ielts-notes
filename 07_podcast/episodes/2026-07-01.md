### Card 1 of 3 — G010: Double Conjunction

---
id: G010
category: grammar
tags: [conjunction, concession, writing, high-priority, critical]
difficulty: Medium
---

**G010 — Double Conjunction: `Although` and `However` Cannot Coexist**

**Core Rule**

`Although` and `However` are both concession markers. Only one can be used per sentence.

- `Although` = subordinating conjunction → joins two clauses in ONE sentence
- `However` = adverb → starts a NEW sentence after a period

This is a direct Chinese grammar transfer (雖然…但是…). It is immediately visible to examiners and affects GRA. Personal error count: 4+ occurrences — Critical priority.

**Common mistakes**

❌ Although education is important, however, it alone cannot solve the problem.
❌ Although prices have risen, however, people still buy.
❌ Although X, but Y (same problem — `although` + `but` also wrong)

**Good examples**

Choose ONE connector — not both:
✅ Although education is important, it alone cannot solve the problem.
✅ Education is important. However, it alone cannot solve the problem.
✅ While education is important, policy changes are also needed.

**Reusable patterns**

| Pattern | Template |
|---|---|
| `Although` | `Although [conceded point], [main claim].` |
| `However` | `[First claim]. However, [contrasting claim].` |
| `While` | `While [conceded point], [main claim].` |
| `Despite` | `Despite [noun phrase], [main claim].` — note: noun phrase only |

**Mini practice (for Rachel to use in dialogue)**

Fix these sentences:
1. Although the government has invested in education, however the results are disappointing.
2. Although fast fashion is cheap, however it creates environmental problems.
3. Although technology connects people, however it also isolates them.

Answers:
1. Although the government has invested in education, the results are disappointing.
2. Although fast fashion is cheap, it creates environmental problems.
3. Although technology connects people, it also isolates them.

### Card 2 of 3 — E001: Concession Expressions

---
id: E001
category: expressions
---

**E001 — Concession Expressions Toolkit**

**Core Rule**

Concession = acknowledge the opposing point, then assert your own. G010 applies: Although and However cannot be in the same sentence.

**Subordinating Conjunctions (same sentence)**

| Expression | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `Although` | `Although [conceded], [main claim].` | Although fast fashion is cheap, it creates serious environmental problems. |
| `While` | `While [conceded], [main claim].` | While some prefer city life, others value rural peace. |
| `Even though` | `Even though [conceded], [main claim].` | Even though the cost is high, many people still buy. |
| `Despite the fact that` | `Despite the fact that [clause], [main claim].` | Despite the fact that regulations exist, enforcement is weak. |

**Adverbs (new sentence)**

| Expression | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `However,` | `[Claim]. However, [contrast].` | Education is important. However, policy changes are also needed. |
| `Nevertheless,` | `[Claim]. Nevertheless, [contrast].` | The risks are real. Nevertheless, the benefits outweigh them. |
| `That said,` | `[Claim]. That said, [contrast].` | The solution is not perfect. That said, it is a step forward. |

**Preposition phrases (noun phrase only)**

| Expression | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `Despite` | `Despite [noun phrase], [claim].` | Despite the high cost, many people still buy. |
| `In spite of` | `In spite of [noun phrase], [claim].` | In spite of government efforts, the problem persists. |

`Despite` trap: takes noun phrase, NOT clause.
❌ Despite the cost is high → ✅ Despite the high cost

**Choosing the right expression**

| Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| One sentence, two clauses | Although / While / Even though |
| Two separate sentences | However / Nevertheless / That said |
| Before a noun phrase | Despite / In spite of |

### Card 3 of 3 — L004: Note Completion Pacing

---
id: L004

**L004 — Note Completion Pacing: Move On, Don't Chase**

**Core Rule**

If you miss one answer, leave a dash (—) and immediately move to the next question.

Do NOT spend time trying to recover the missed answer while the recording continues. You will lose TWO answers instead of one.

**Why it matters**

Note completion (especially in S4) runs at a continuous pace. Losing focus on one question for too long causes a cascade of errors. One blank is recoverable; three missed in a row is not.

**Strategy**

1. Pre-read: read ALL questions before the recording starts
2. During: write when you have the answer; dash when you don't
3. When you miss one: say "skip" in your head and look at the NEXT question immediately
4. On review: go back to the dash — sometimes context from later answers helps

**Pacing rule for S4**

S4 is a monologue. Questions follow the recording in order.
- If you're still on question 35 but the recording is clearly at question 37 → skip 35 and 36, catch 37

**Cambridge-style example (for Rachel to use in dialogue)**

Imagine this note completion section:
"The seminar covers three main topics: (31) _____, data analysis, and (32) _____."

If you miss (31): leave a dash, immediately look at (32). After the recording, try to recall (31) from context.

## Speaking 練習主題

**類型：** Part 3（週三 = 社會觀點）

**題目：** Should governments control the amount of clothing people buy, or is it up to individuals to make responsible choices?

**回答要點：**
- 誰的責任更大（政府 vs 個人）
- 政府可以做什麼（regulation / tax on fast fashion）
- 個人可以做什麼（buy second-hand / buy less）
- 哪個方式長遠效果更好

**Sample Answer (for Rachel to model):**

I think both have a role to play, but governments carry the greater responsibility. Individuals can choose to buy less or shop second-hand, but without regulations, the fashion industry has no reason to change its production model. Governments, for example, could impose a tax on fast fashion items or require companies to disclose their environmental impact. Although individual choices matter, they rarely achieve the scale needed to make a difference. In the long run, structural policy change tends to be far more effective than relying on personal willpower alone.
