**對應草稿：** drafts/task2/language-loss_2026-06-26.md

## 範本全文

Every year, a significant number of languages disappear from the world. Some argue this is not a matter of concern, as fewer languages would make global communication more convenient. I strongly disagree with this view, as it overlooks two fundamental values that linguistic diversity provides.

Firstly, language is far more than a tool for daily communication — it is the medium through which human knowledge and cultural heritage are preserved. When a language is lost, the centuries of wisdom encoded within it disappear as well. The ancient Egyptian hieroglyphic script is a powerful example: without scholars painstakingly decoding this extinct writing system, the knowledge inscribed on temple walls and across mummified texts would have remained permanently inaccessible. A lost language, in this sense, represents the permanent destruction of a portion of human history.

Furthermore, different languages capture concepts and nuances that others simply cannot replicate. The Inuit people, for instance, have dozens of distinct words for different types of snow — a linguistic richness that reflects their deep relationship with their environment. No single dominant language, however widely spoken, could fully replace this precision. Allowing languages to disappear would mean stripping away unique ways of perceiving and describing reality, ultimately narrowing the range of human thought and creativity.

While it is true that sharing a common language would ease certain practical interactions, this convenience is far outweighed by what would be permanently lost. A world of fewer languages would be a world of narrower thinking and diminished cultural memory. Rather than accepting this erosion as inevitable, we should actively work to document and revitalise endangered languages before they are gone.

### 這篇好在哪（我要學的結構/手法）

- **Intro 直接改寫題目 + 表態**：第一句改寫題目，第二句承認對立立場，第三句清楚表態 + 預告兩個理由——三句話，結構完整。
- **對「便利」前提的處理**：沒有在 body 裡完全忽略對方的主張，而是放在結論段用讓步句處理（"While it is true that..."）。這是應對 "to what extent" 題型的正確手法。
- **Body 1 的例子有力**：古埃及例子不是泛泛而談，直接說「沒有學者解碼，那些知識就永遠無法取得」——把「語言消失 = 知識消失」的邏輯說清楚了。
- **Body 2 的例子來自老師建議（愛斯基摩人的雪）**：直接說明「某些概念只有特定語言才能精確表達」，比音樂/歌曲的例子更直接支持論點。
- **結論不只是重複**：用 "narrower thinking and diminished cultural memory" 提出新角度，再呼應行動（revitalise），不是只抄 intro。

### 值得學的表達（自然、可借用）

- `is not a matter of concern` — 說某事「不值得擔心」的正式說法，比 "is not important" 更精確
- `it overlooks [two fundamental values]` — 說對方「忽略了」某個重點，適合 disagree 類題型的 intro 結尾
- `is the medium through which...` — 說某事物是傳遞某東西的「媒介」，結構清楚
- `encoded within it` — 說知識「嵌入/儲存在語言裡」，比 "contained in" 更有意象
- `painstakingly` — 形容費力、仔細地做某事，修飾 decoding 很自然
- `capture concepts and nuances` — 說語言能「捕捉概念與細微差異」，標準說法
- `simply cannot replicate` — 強調無法複製，simply 加強語氣
- `far outweigh` — 說某事「遠遠超過」另一件事的重量，用於讓步後反駁（convenience is far outweighed by...）
- `this erosion` — 把語言消失比作「侵蝕」，呼應前文，比重複說 "languages disappearing" 更有文采
- `revitalise` — 讓語言「重新活化」，比 "protect" 或 "save" 更積極、更準確

### 連接詞 / 句型片語（可直接套用）

| 片語 | 位置/功能 |
|---|---|
| `Some argue [that]... I strongly disagree with this view, as...` | Intro — 承認對立立場 + 表態，一氣呵成 |
| `Firstly, ... is far more than [X] — it is [Y]` | Body 開頭 — 先否定窄義，再給出更廣的定義 |
| `[Noun] is a powerful example: without..., ... would have remained [adj]` | 例子句型 — 說明若沒有這個條件，結果會是什麼（反事實）|
| `for instance, ... have dozens of distinct words for...` | 例子引入 — 比 "for example" 更書面，接具體數字更有說服力 |
| `No single [X], however [adj], could fully [V]` | 反駁句 — 承認對立立場的某個特點，但說它仍不夠 |
| `While it is true that [讓步], [反駁]` | 結論段讓步 — 正面承認對方有一點道理，但說你的理由更重要 |
| `is far outweighed by` | 讓步後反駁的核心動詞，說代價遠大於好處 |
| `Rather than [V-ing], we should [V]` | 結論行動句 — 比 "In conclusion, we should" 更有力度 |
